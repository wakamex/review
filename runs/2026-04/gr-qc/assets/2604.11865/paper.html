Beyond the Cosmological Constant: Breaking the Geometric Degeneracy of f​(Q)f(Q) cosmology via Redshift-Space Distortions

Abstract

We present a rigorous theoretical and observational analysis of the Hybrid f​(Q)f(Q) class of models by including the late-time modifying 1/Q1/Q term. After deriving strict viability conditions from the analytical expansion history, we show that preserving early-universe structure formation dictates that the linear coupling be exactly unity. This fixes the background of the Hybrid model into a geometric degeneracy with Λ\LambdaCDM which is confirmed explicitly through MCMC analysis with the latest background-only probes. The physical novelty of this model is manifest in the perturbation sector, where the geometric coupling breaks the background degeneracy and induces a late-time suppression of the effective gravitational constant Ge​f​f<GNG_{eff}<G_{N}. Consequently, the inclusion of RSD data reveals an amplitude compensation mechanism, by which the matching of the signature f​σ8f\sigma_{8} of the data causes the clustering amplitude σ8\sigma_{8} to inflate under weaker gravity. Statistical model comparison through AIC/DIC demonstrates that incorporating growth data yields a moderate to weak preference for the Hybrid model keeping the background cosmology intact. This provides a physically bounded alternative to Λ\LambdaCDM with a falsifiable signature in the large scale structure, directly testable by the next generation of galaxy surveys.

I Introduction

The Λ\LambdaCDM model provides a minimal, statistically robust description of our Universe consistent with modern observations ranging from Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) [18] to the Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) [2] to the Large-scale Structure (LSS) [3] of late-time. The cosmological constant Λ\Lambda, which is the models answer to the observed cosmic acceleration [44, 41], suffers from the catastrophic vacuum energy fine-tuning problem [47]. This is one of the many gaps in the conventionally formulated concordance model, which together motivate the search for a more consistent picture of the Universe. One such picture arises from the geometric modifications in the form of Symmetric Teleparallel Equivalent of General Relativity (STEGR) [39, 12, 10, 11, 26], whose models intend to explain the late-time acceleration through pure geometry without a need for a tunable vacuum field. In this work, we focus on the extended STEGR or the f​(Q)f(Q) theory which explains gravitational effects entirely through the non-metricity tensor Qα​β​γQ_{\alpha\beta\gamma} and scalar QQ of a flat, torsion-free connection. The f​(Q)f(Q) theory has generated a significant amount of attention [9, 34, 11, 36, 35, 8, 33, 20, 25, 5, 22, 24, 46, 23, 6, 37, 15, 16, 38, 13, 30, 17, 27, 32, 28] due to the strictly second-order field equations, avoiding the Ostrogradsky instabilities that plague higher-order metric theories.

In the coincident gauge the non-metricity scalar for the spatially flat FLRW metric evaluates to Q=6​H2Q=6H^{2} where H≡H​(z)H\equiv H(z) is the Hubble parameter. Realizing that HH increases monotonically with increasing redshift zz, one can patch together a simple non-trivial model of the form f​(Q)∼Q+1/Qf(Q)\sim Q+1/Q which only modifies the late-time dynamics. It is clear that this gives back GR like behavior at early times. We call this the Hybrid model which has been previously explored in the literature [11, 42, 29, 7].

In this work, we wish to express the importance of deriving analytical viability conditions before injecting the model into a MCMC sampler, especially for non-trivial cosmologies. Without prior knowledge of the parameter boundaries and the forbidden regions, a sampler can enter unphysical regions and give rise to instabilities. Hence, we start by deriving the mathematically viable constraints for the Hybrid model and show that in order to preserve high redshift behavior, the linear coupling has to be exactly unity. This causes the early time behavior of the model to become degenerate with Λ\LambdaCDM. The late-time probes including Cosmic Chronometers, Type Ia Supernovae and Baryon Acoustic Oscillations do not break this background degeneracy. We thus include growth data invoking the linear perturbation sector, and find that the Hybrid coupling suppresses the effective Gravitational constant Ge​f​fG_{eff} at late-times. However, the clustering amplitude σ8\sigma_{8} is increased in order to accommodate the observed values f​σ8f\sigma_{8}.

This manuscript is organized as follows: section II gives the convention and equations for f​(Q)f(Q) gravity followed by section III that outline analytical constraints as well as model branch selection. We also show the linear coupling, α1=1\alpha_{1}=1 here. section IV then mentions the observational datasets and the statistical methodology used. section V demonstrates the results of background and perturbation analyses. Finally, we conclude our manuscript in section VI and discuss the feasibility of the model in light of the upcoming next-generation surveys.
