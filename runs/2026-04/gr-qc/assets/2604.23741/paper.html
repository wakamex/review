On gravitating dyonic configurations in nonlinear electrodynamics

K. A. Bronnikov,a,b,c,1 S. V. Bolokhov,b,2 G. S. Nurbakova,d and B. Tynyshbayd

We consider static, spherically symmetric configurations of nonlinear electromagnetic fields with Lagrangians L​(f)L(f), where f=Fμ​ν​Fμ​νf=F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu}, in general relativity (GR) and other metric theories of gravity. The corresponding exact solutions are well known in the framework of GR in cases where only an electric charge (qeq_{e}) or a magnetic charge (qmq_{m}) are present, but only a few solutions in particular examples of L​(f)L(f) are known for dyonic systems with both nonzero qeq_{e} and qmq_{m}. We study the properties of such systems in the special case of equal electric and magnetic charges and, assuming a correct Maxwell limit of L​(f)L(f), show that there always exists such a configuration of the electromagnetic field that the invariant ff is zero in the whole space. It leads to the existence of the corresponding families of solutions both in GR in the presence of other sources of gravity (like fluids or scalar fields) and in a wide range of extended theories of gravity (e.g., scalar-tensor and f​(R)f(R) gravity), in which the nonlinear electromagnetic field behaves in an especially simple manner.

1 Introduction

In general relativity (GR) and its extensions, nonlinear electrodynamics (NED) is known to be quite a popular source of gravity since, on one hand, strong electromagnetic fields are inevitably nonlinear due to particle interactions, and, on the other, they are able to create promising space-time geometries, such as nonsingular black holes and solitonlike or starlike configurations without horizons.

The first examples of NED theories apparently belong to Born and Infeld whose goal was to remove infinite electromagnetic self-energy of a point charge [1], and to Heisenberg and Euler whose theory was motivated by the physics of particle interactions [2]. Much later, the NED paradigm was further developed by Plebanski [3] in the framework of special relativity.

The subsequent development has included a huge number of studies of self-gravitating systems with NED as a source of gravity in GR and its alternatives, including searches for exact solutions, their thorough studies including their stability and thermodynamic properties, particle motion in nonlinear electromagnetic fields in flat and curved spaces, NED causality and unitarity properties, and so on, see for reviews, e.g., [4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10] and references therein.

In this paper, we focus on dyonic self-gravitating NED systems with the NED Lagrangian equal to −L​(f),f=Fμ​ν​Fμ​ν-L(f),\ f=F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu}, possessing both electric (qeq_{e}) and magnetic (qmq_{m}) charges without fixing a theory of gravity but assuming a static, spherically symmetric space-time.

It turns out that dyonic solutions to NED-gravity equations are much harder to obtain than purely electric or magnetic ones. Thus, in the framework of GR, solutions with only qe≠0q_{e}\neq 0 or only qm≠0q_{m}\neq 0 are easily found with arbitrary L​(f)L(f) [12, 11, 13, 14], though electric solutions are more readily obtained using a Hamiltonian-like formulation of NED, the so-called P-framework [12, 15, 11], while magnetic ones do not need such a re-formulation. Unlike that, for dyonic systems, solutions in GR have been obtained only for specific examples of NED theories, to say nothing on extensions of GR with more complicated equations.

Indeed, consider L​(f)L(f) theory in an arbitrary static, spherically symmetric space-time with the metric

where uu is a non-specified radial coordinate and r​(u)r(u) the spherical radius. Then the only admissible components of the Maxwell tensor Fμ​νF_{\mu\nu} are Ft​r=−Fr​tF_{tr}=-F_{rt} (a radial electric field) and Fθ​ϕ=−Fϕ​θF_{\theta\phi}=-F_{\phi\theta} (a radial magnetic field). The electromagnetic field equations ∇μ(Lf​Fμ​ν)=0\nabla_{\mu}(L_{f}F^{\mu\nu})=0 and ∇μFμ​ν∗=0\nabla_{\mu}{}^{*}F^{\mu\nu}=0 (Fμ​ν∗{}^{*}F^{\mu\nu} being the dual field) then lead to

If qm=0q_{m}=0, a similar simple expression exists in the P-formulation of the theory [12, 15, 11]. If both charges are nonzero, then, for given L​(f)L(f), as noted in [10], Eq. (3) can be considered as either a transcendental (in general) equation for the function f​(r)f(r) or an expression of rr as a function of ff. 3 A general observation for
NED-gravity equations

From the above examples (and probably others) it follows that dyonic NED-GR solutions are rather hard to obtain: even if f​(r)f(r) is known, finding the metric function A​(r)A(r) requires integration that, in general, cannot be implemented in known functions and needs using numerical methods. This concerns static, spherically symmetric systems in GR, to say nothing on less symmetric systems and extended theories of gravity. However, each time it has turned out that in the case of equal electric and magnetic charges there is a particularly simple solution actually canceling the nonlinearity of NED and reducing the electromagnetic field to its self-dual Maxwell configuration. Let us show that this did not happen by chance but leads to quite a general observation.

Suppose that the NED Lagrangian L​(f)L(f) has a correct Maxwell limit, so that L​(f)=f+o​(f)L(f)=f+o(f) at small ff, and admits a Taylor expansion in a neighborhood of f=0f=0,

L​(f)=f+∑k=2∞ak​fk,ak=const.L(f)=f+\sum_{k=2}^{\infty}a_{k}f^{k},\hskip 28.45274pta_{k}={\rm const}. (29)

Consider a static, spherically symmetric configuration of the electromagnetic field in a space-time with the metric (1), then Eq. (3) is valid, and in the case qe2=qm2=q2q_{e}^{2}=q_{m}^{2}=q^{2} it takes the form

12​f​r4=q2​p​(f),p​(f):=1−Lf−2.{{\displaystyle\frac{1}{2}}}fr^{4}=q^{2}p(f),\hskip 28.45274ptp(f):=1-L_{f}^{-2}. (30)

Due to (29), p​(f)p(f) is also a smooth function near f=0f=0, and we can write

p​(f)=∑k=0∞pk​fk,pk=const.p(f)=\sum_{k=0}^{\infty}p_{k}f^{k},\ \ \ \ p_{k}={\rm const}. (31)

Since Lf=1L_{f}=1 at f=0f=0, we have p​(0)=0p(0)=0, so it follows p0=0p_{0}=0 in (31), and the sum begins with p1p_{1}. Thus Eq. (30) may be rewritten as

f​(r4−2​q2​∑k=1∞pk​fk−1)=0.f\bigg(r^{4}-2q^{2}\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}p_{k}f^{k-1}\bigg)=0. (32)

This equation evidently has the solution f≡0f\equiv 0. We conclude that under the above conditions, there always exists this solution, with f≡0f\equiv 0 in the whole space, and, in particular, in GR it leads to the RNdS solution with the metric function (10) (where qe2+qm2=2​q2q_{e}^{2}+q_{m}^{2}=2q^{2}). Meanwhile, other solutions to Eq. (32) are not excluded since there is no such a uniqueness theorem, and we have really presented such solutions in our Examples 3–6.

It should be stressed that this observation is not restricted to GR or to electrovacuum space-times. One can notice that Eqs. (1)–(3) are valid without fixing the choice of the radial coordinate uu, therefore, the above observation is quite universal and is not restricted to systems with Ttt=TuuT^{t}_{t}=T^{u}_{u} or to those parametrized by the radial coordinate rr. Also, there is no restriction to asymptotically flat configurations, as is already clear from using Λ\Lambda in the Einstein equations (4).

In the framework of GR we can readily assert that many existing static, spherically symmetric solutions with a Maxwell electromagnetic field may be re-interpreted as special solutions sourced by arbitrary NED with L​(f)L(f) having a correct Maxwell limit. Among them are systems sourced by charged fluids with any equations of state (see, e.g., [21, 22] and references therein), though in systems with charged matter, at such a re-interpretation one has to replace electric or magnetic charges and currents with combinations of equal electric and magnetic charges and currents. This also concerns systems with massless, massive or self-interacting scalar fields of any nature coupled to a Maxwell field (see, e.g., [23, 24, 25]), including black hole and wormhole solutions.

The same is true for solutions with Maxwell fields in other metric theories of gravity, such as, for example, scalar-tensor and f​(R)f(R) theories [26, 27, 28]. One can recall, in particular, that the most well-known scalar-tensor theories (STT), belonging to the Bergmann-Wagoner-Nordtvedt class, can be formulated in two conformally related space-times: the Jordan (original and preferred) conformal frame and the Einstein frame where the theory looks as GR with a scalar field minimally coupled with curvature but in general nonminimally coupled to matter. It can be explicitly shown that, even though L​(f)L(f) NED theories are not conformally invariant, their solution with f≡0f\equiv 0, with effectively Maxwell behavior of the theory, is simultaneously valid in both conformal frames. Furthermore, since f​(R)f(R) theories admit a reformulation as STT [29, 30], the same is true for them as well.

It is, however, not so evident whether or not our observation on L​(f)L(f) NED with f≡0f\equiv 0 can be extended to solutions of STT and f​(R)f(R) gravity involving conformal continuations [31, 32], in which the whole Einstein-frame manifold is conformally related to only a region of the Jordan-frame manifold, as happens, for example, in wormhole solutions of GR with nonminimally coupled scalar fields [26, 33] and in the exceptional wormhole solution of the Brans-Dicke theory [34]. This issue needs a further study.

4 Concluding remarks

In conclusion, we would like to designate possible further extensions of our main result, the repeated emergence of solutions with f≡0f\equiv 0 in static, spherically symmetric space-times inherent to various NED theories, independently from the underlying theory of gravity and the presence of other kinds of matter non-interacting with the electromagnetic field.

It is quite natural to seek possible extensions to other space-time symmetries: for planar and pseudospherical ones an extension looks quite straightforward, less evident it should be for cylindrical symmetry, and it seems rather problematic for axial symmetry.

Similar points can be studied in multidimensional generalizations of NED, in particular, in spaces of even dimension D=2​nD=2n.

An interesting situation is expected with NED theories whose Lagrangians depend on two electromagnetic invariants, f=Fμ​ν​Fμ​νf=F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu} and g2=(Fμ​ν∗​Fμ​ν)2g^{2}=({}^{*}F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu})^{2}. Such theories possess features quite different from those of L​(f)L(f). Thus, in L​(f)L(f) theories it is impossible to obtain dyonic black hole solutions with a regular center, while in L​(f,g)L(f,g) theories such examples are known [35, 36]. Not less important is the existence of duality-invariant theories in this class, allowing for obtaining dyonic solutions with an arbitrary combination of qeq_{e} and qmq_{m} by duality rotations, starting from an electric or magnetic solution. This property is well known for the Born-Infeld theory [1] (see also [37] and references therein) with the Lagrangian

L(f,g)=b2(−1+1+2​f/b2−2​g2/b4,b=const\hskip-5.0ptL(f,g)=b^{2}(-1+\sqrt{1\!+2f/b^{2}\!-2g^{2}/b^{4}},\ \ b={\rm const}

(Eq. (11) is its truncated version); it also takes place in the recently proposed ModMax (Modified Maxwell) theory [38, 39] with the Lagrangian

L​(f,g)=f​cosh⁡γ−f2+g2​sinh⁡γ,γ=const,L(f,g)=f\cosh\gamma\!-\sqrt{f^{2}\!+g^{2}}\sinh\gamma,\ \ \gamma={\rm const},

which, in addition, possesses conformal invariance. It looks quite intriguing how and under which conditions there would emerge solutions with f=0f=0 where, in general, the second invariant g2g^{2} must not vanish.

Thus there remain a number of questions of interest connected with possible simple solutions in quite sophisticated theories.
