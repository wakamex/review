New Almost Universal Metrics

Metin Gürses gurses@fen.bilkent.edu.tr Department of Mathematics, Bilkent University, 06800 Ankara, Türkiye    Tahsin Çağrı Şişman tahsin.c.sisman@gmail.com Department of Astronautical Engineering, University of Turkish Aeronautical Association, 06790 Ankara, Türkiye    Bayram Tekin bayram.tekin@bilkent.edu.tr Department of Physics, Bilkent University, 06800 Ankara, Türkiye
Abstract

Plane waves and p​ppp-waves are well-known universal metrics that solve all metric-based gravitational field equations. Similarly, the Kerr-Schild-Kundt class of metrics is almost universal: all metric-based gravitational field equations reduce to a linear scalar partial differential equation that always admits a solution. Here, we add a new member to this class of metrics and show that nonzero constant curvature pp-wave metrics are also almost universal. They reduce the generic gravity field equations to those of cosmological Einstein-Maxwell theory with null dust. The background of the pp-waves has the topology ℝ1,1×S2\mathbb{R}^{1,1}\times S^{2} and provides the missing partner to the Nariai metric with dS2×S2{\rm dS}^{2}\times S^{2} and the Bertotti-Robinson metric with AdS2×S2{\rm AdS}^{2}\times S^{2} topologies. These quantum-protected metrics are of clear interest. We exemplify our results by using the quadratic and cubic gravity theories.

Introduction–Higher-curvature contributions, built from the Riemann tensor and its covariant derivatives, are expected to play a central role in any viable quantum theory of gravity. In particular, in the low-energy limit of string theory, the gravitational Lagrangian contains infinitely many curvature invariants and their derivatives. The corresponding field equations are typically extremely complicated and, in general, do not admit generic Einstein metrics as solutions.

Nevertheless, certain special metrics in General Relativity retain an extraordinary property: they continue to solve the field equations of all higher–derivative gravity theories constructed from curvature and its covariant derivatives, independently of the detailed form of the Lagrangian. Understanding these “quantum-protected” or universal metrics is therefore of considerable conceptual and technical interest.

Beginning with Deser [1] and subsequent works [2]–[5], plane waves have been shown to be completely insensitive to quantum corrections: all higher-order curvature contributions vanish identically in their effective field equations. For a long time, plane waves were believed to be the only universal metric. This picture changed when p​ppp-waves were shown to share the same property [6, 7, 8].

Both plane waves and p​ppp-waves belong to the Kerr–Schild family and enjoy additional structural simplifications. The metrics to be studied here have analogous properties; let us recap some properties of the p​ppp-waves and Kerr-Schild-Kundt (KSK) metrics. The p​ppp-waves take their simplest form in the Brinkmann coordinates,

d​s2=2​d​u​d​v+d​x2+d​y2+2​V​(u,x,y)​d​u2,ds^{2}=2\,du\,dv+dx^{2}+dy^{2}+2V(u,x,y)\,du^{2}, (1)

for which the Einstein field equations reduce to □​V=0\Box V=0, where □\Box denotes the Beltrami–Laplace operator of the flat background, that is V=0V=0 in (1). The plane waves correspond to the choice V=Sa​b​(u)​xa​xbV=S_{ab}(u)x^{a}x^{b} with trace​S=0\mathrm{trace}\,S=0; hence, the Einstein field equations are satisfied identically. Therefore, the plane wave metrics are said to be universal. A defining property of p​ppp-waves is that the null vector λμ=∂μu\lambda_{\mu}=\partial_{\mu}u is covariantly constant: ∇μλν=0\nabla_{\mu}\lambda_{\nu}=0. Such metrics do not automatically satisfy the field equations of a generic gravity theory; hence, they are not universal. However, they are almost universal since the field equations of a generic gravity theory reduce to,

∑n=1Nan​□n​V=0.\sum_{n=1}^{N}a_{n}\,\Box^{n}V=0. (2)

where ana_{n} depend on the coupling constants of the chosen higher–curvature theory. In this flat background, the Kerr–Schild metric

gμ​νAU=ημ​ν+2​V​(u,xa)​λμ​λν,g_{\mu\nu}^{\mathrm{AU}}=\eta_{\mu\nu}+2V(u,x^{a})\,\lambda_{\mu}\lambda_{\nu}, (3)

naturally distinguishes between naturally universal (Minkowski) backgrounds and almost universal (AU) metrics, which obey one additional scalar equation of the form (2).

Beyond the flat spacetime, de Sitter (dS) and anti-de Sitter (AdS) spacetimes are also naturally universal. This observation led to the generalization of almost universal metrics to KSK metrics [8]–[10, 11, 12, 13, 16] with constant–curvature backgrounds, which take the form

gμ​ν=g¯μ​ν+2​V​λμ​λν,g_{\mu\nu}=\bar{g}_{\mu\nu}+2V\,\lambda_{\mu}\lambda_{\nu}, (4)

where g¯μ​ν\bar{g}_{\mu\nu} is the (A)dS metric, and the null vector λμ\lambda_{\mu} satisfies

∇μλν=ξμ​λν+ξν​λμ,\nabla_{\mu}\lambda_{\nu}=\xi_{\mu}\lambda_{\nu}+\xi_{\nu}\lambda_{\mu}, (5)

with ξμ\xi_{\mu}, defined by this equation, is orthogonal to λμ\lambda_{\mu}; and λμ​∂μV=0\lambda^{\mu}\partial_{\mu}V=0. For the generic class of theories defined by

I=∫dD​x​−g​f​(g,Riem,∇Riem,…),I=\int d^{D}x\,\sqrt{-g}\,f\!\left(g,\mathrm{Riem},\nabla\mathrm{Riem},\ldots\right), (6)

where ff is a smooth function. The field equations of (6) take the form

Gα​β+Λ0​gα​β+Hα​β=κ​Tα​β,G_{\alpha\beta}+\Lambda_{0}g_{\alpha\beta}+H_{\alpha\beta}=\kappa T_{\alpha\beta}, (7)

where Gα​β≡Rα​β−12​gα​β​RG_{\alpha\beta}\equiv R_{\alpha\beta}-\frac{1}{2}g_{\alpha\beta}R, and Hα​βH_{\alpha\beta} represents all the higher derivative terms. The KSK ansätz (4)reduces the source-free field equations to: (i) algebraic relations among the couplings, the cosmological constant, and the background curvature, and (ii) a single linear partial differential equation for VV of the form

∑n=1Nan​□n​V=0,\sum_{n=1}^{N}a_{n}\,\Box^{n}V=0, (8)

hence they are almost universal.

Now, we move to the discussion of p​ppp-wave metrics with non-flat transverse two-space. Recently, a new family of four-dimensional solutions of quadratic gravity was constructed in [17] that are of the form

d​s2=2​d​u​d​v+ha​b​(xc)​d​xa​d​xb+2​V​(u,xa)​d​u2,ds^{2}=2\,du\,dv+h_{ab}(x^{c})\,dx^{a}dx^{b}+2V(u,x^{a})\,du^{2}, (9)

where (u,v)(u,v) are null coordinates and ha​bh_{ab} (a=2,3a=2,3) is the metric of a two-dimensional constant-curvature surface S2S^{2} or H2H^{2}. In particular, ha​bh_{ab} depends only on xax^{a} and not on uu or vv. These metrics do not solve Einstein’s equations with a cosmological constant and are therefore genuinely non-Einsteinian p​ppp-wave solutions of quadratic gravity. Let us first focus on the corresponding background metric,

d​s2=2​d​u​d​v+h22​(d​x2)2+2​h23​d​x2​d​x3+h33​(d​x3)2,ds^{2}=2\,du\,dv+h_{22}(dx^{2})^{2}+2h_{23}\,dx^{2}dx^{3}+h_{33}\,(dx^{3})^{2}, (10)

to show that it is almost universal and solves the cosmological Einstein-Maxwell equations. Its Riemann tensor has a nontrivial part only in the transverse two-space:

R¯a​b​c​d=R¯2​(ha​c​hb​d−ha​d​hb​c),\bar{R}_{abcd}=\frac{\bar{R}}{2}\left(h_{ac}h_{bd}-h_{ad}h_{bc}\right), (11)

where we defined Λ\Lambda in the last equation in terms of the constant scalar curvature. With a null λμ=δμ0\lambda_{\mu}=\delta_{\mu}^{0} satisfying ∇μλν=0\nabla_{\mu}\lambda_{\nu}=0, the metric tensor can be written in the form

g¯μ​ν=nμ​λν+nν​λμ+hμ​ν,\bar{g}_{\mu\nu}=n_{\mu}\,\lambda_{\nu}+n_{\nu}\,\lambda_{\mu}+h_{\mu\nu}, (13)

where nμ=δμ1n_{\mu}=\delta_{\mu}^{1} satisfies ∇μnν=0\nabla_{\mu}\,n_{\nu}=0. Here, hμ​νh_{\mu\nu} has the non-zero components ha​bh_{ab}; therefore, it satisfies λμ​hμ​ν=0\lambda^{\mu}h_{\mu\nu}=0 and nμ​hμ​ν=0n^{\mu}h_{\mu\nu}=0. Let us show that g¯μ​ν\bar{g}_{\mu\nu} is a solution to the cosmological Einstein-Maxwell theory with the action

since λμ​nμ=1\lambda^{\mu}\,n_{\mu}=1 and nμ​λν+nν​λν=g¯μ​ν−hμ​νn_{\mu}\,\lambda_{\nu}+n_{\nu}\lambda_{\nu}=\bar{g}_{\mu\nu}-h_{\mu\nu}. Using this result together with the Ricci tensor and scalar curvature from (12) in the cosmological Einstein-Maxwell field equations Gμ​ν+Λ0​gμ​ν=κ​Tμ​ν(F)G_{\mu\nu}+\Lambda_{0}g_{\mu\nu}=\kappa T_{\mu\nu}^{(F)} yields

Λ=Λ0=κ​ε22=κ​E22.\Lambda=\Lambda_{0}=\kappa\frac{\varepsilon^{2}}{2}=\kappa\frac{E^{2}}{2}. (19)

Therefore, the electromagnetic energy density determines the cosmological constant.

With the topology ℝ1,1×S2\mathbb{R}^{1,1}\times S^{2} , g¯μ​ν\bar{g}_{\mu\nu} is a critical point between the Nariai solution dS2×S2{\rm dS}^{2}\times S^{2} and the Bertotti-Robinson solution AdS2×S2{\rm AdS}^{2}\times S^{2}; further properties of this metric were discussed in [18].

Theorem 1.

The metric

d​s2=2​d​u​d​v+ha​b​(xc)​d​xa​d​xb,ds^{2}=2\,{\rm d}u\,{\rm d}v+h_{ab}\left(x^{c}\right)\,{\rm d}x^{a}\,{\rm d}x^{b}, (20)

satisfies the cosmological Einstein-Maxwell field equations for the electromagnetic four-potential

Aμ=2​Λκ​v​λμ,A_{\mu}=\sqrt{\frac{2\Lambda}{\kappa}}\,v\lambda_{\mu}, (21)

where λμ=∂μu\lambda_{\mu}=\partial_{\mu}u, and Λ\Lambda is a positive cosmological constant.

In [19], we showed that (20) solves the generic gravity field equations (7); hence, it is almost universal. This property means that for the metric (20), any rank-two symmetric tensor built from the metric, its curvature, and covariant derivatives is a linear combination of g¯μ​ν\bar{g}_{\mu\nu} and hμ​νh_{\mu\nu}. Therefore, we have the following theorem:

Theorem 2.

For any generic gravity theory, Hμ​νH_{\mu\nu} in (7) representing all the higher derivative terms takes the form

where e0e_{0} and e1e_{1} are constants that depend on the theory parameters and the cosmological constant Λ\Lambda.

Then we have the next theorem:

Conclusions

Universal and almost universal metrics studied so far in the literature are typically Einsteinian, i.e., they solve field equations derived from the Einstein–Hilbert action. In this Letter, we have identified a new class of almost universal metrics based on pp-waves with constant-curvature transverse spaces, whose backgrounds are themselves non-Einsteinian. For generic higher–curvature theories of gravity, these metrics reduce the field equations to two algebraic relations among the cosmological constant, the curvature of the constant–curvature surface S2S^{2} or H2H^{2}, and the coupling constants of the theory, together with a single linear differential equation for the Kerr–Schild profile function VV. We have explicitly demonstrated this for quadratic and cubic gravity and outlined how the construction generalizes to more complicated higher–derivative theories.
