Abstract

We show that the diffusion-based models proposed in Refs.¬Ý[10, 11] within the framework of Unimodular Gravity (UG) to alleviate the H0H_{0} tension are incompatible with the second law of thermodynamics. Starting from the Gibbs equation for a pressureless matter fluid, we derive a general thermodynamic admissibility condition for the Œõ\LambdaCDM++diffusion class in UG, demonstrating that the second law requires QÀô<0\dot{Q}<0, independently of the specific form of the diffusion function Q‚Äã(t)Q(t). This condition implies that energy must flow from the effective cosmological term Œõeff\Lambda_{\rm eff} into the matter sector, rather than in the opposite direction. We then establish a no-go theorem: no choice of Q‚Äã(t)Q(t) can simultaneously satisfy the second law and generate the growing effective cosmological term ŒõÀôeff>0\dot{\Lambda}_{\rm eff}>0 required to alleviate the H0H_{0} tension. We confirm this result explicitly for the models of Perez, Sudarsky, and Wilson-Ewing¬Ý[10] and Landau et al.¬Ý[11], noting that the former explicitly identify ŒõÀôeff>0\dot{\Lambda}_{\rm eff}>0 as a necessary feature of their proposal without addressing its thermodynamic implications. The incompatibility is therefore structural and applies to the entire class of Œõ\LambdaCDM++diffusion models in UG with a pressureless matter component.

I Introduction

The persistent tension between the value of the Hubble constant H0H_{0} inferred from Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB) observations within the Œõ\LambdaCDM framework and the value measured directly from local distance indicators remains one of the most pressing open problems in modern cosmology¬Ý[1, 2, 3, 4]. In this context, Unimodular Gravity¬Ý[5, 6, 7] has attracted considerable attention as a natural framework to address this tension, since it allows for violations of the local conservation of the energy-momentum tensor without introducing any inconsistency in the field equations¬Ý[8, 9].

The central idea shared by the proposals of Refs.¬Ý[10, 11] is that a diffusion process transfers energy from the matter sector into an effective dark energy component, modifying the late-time cosmological evolution and thereby reconciling the CMB-inferred and locally measured values of H0H_{0}. Both proposals are instances of what we shall call the Œõ\LambdaCDM++diffusion model in UG: a spatially flat FLRW universe containing pressureless matter and an effective cosmological term Œõeff‚Äã(t)=Œõ0+Q‚Äã(t)\Lambda_{\rm eff}(t)=\Lambda_{0}+Q(t), where Œõ0\Lambda_{0} is a constant of integration and Q‚Äã(t)Q(t) is a diffusion function encoding the non-conservation of the energy-momentum tensor. This model reduces exactly to standard Œõ\LambdaCDM of General Relativity when Q=0Q=0. We stress that a defining feature of Œõ\LambdaCDM is that cold dark matter is pressureless, i.e., it has vanishing equation of state wcdm=0w_{\rm cdm}=0¬Ý[12, 13]; any model in which the dark matter component acquires an effective non-zero pressure falls outside this class by definition. The two proposals¬Ý[10, 11] differ only in the specific functional form assumed for Q‚Äã(t)Q(t). We emphasize that the results of this comment, including the impossibility theorem proved in Sec.¬ÝIV, apply exclusively to this class of models and should not be interpreted as a general obstruction to the H0H_{0} tension within the broader UG framework.

These proposals represent an important and creative line of research, and the results obtained are encouraging from the observational point of view. However, we note that none of these works verifies whether the adopted diffusion functions are compatible with the second law of thermodynamics. A thermodynamic analysis of UG with diffusion has recently been carried out in Ref.¬Ý[14], where a general condition T‚Äãd‚ÄãS=‚àíV‚Äãd‚ÄãQT\,dS=-V\,dQ was derived and applied to the barotropic and continuous spontaneous localization diffusion models; however, that work does not discuss the H0H_{0} tension and does not analyze the phenomenological models of Refs.¬Ý[10, 11], which are precisely the ones proposed to resolve it. The present comment fills this gap by applying the thermodynamic admissibility condition to those specific models, showing that the very configurations required to alleviate the H0H_{0} tension are thermodynamically inadmissible, and that this incompatibility is in fact structural: no choice of Q‚Äã(t)Q(t) within the Œõ\LambdaCDM++diffusion model can simultaneously satisfy the second law and produce a growing Œõeff\Lambda_{\rm eff}, as required by the H0H_{0} resolution mechanism.

IV Discussion and an impossibility theorem

The analysis presented in the preceding section shows that both proposals in Refs.¬Ý[10, 11] require QÀô>0\dot{Q}>0, i.e., an increasing Œõeff‚Äã(t)\Lambda_{\rm eff}(t), in order to alleviate the H0H_{0} tension. This requirement is in direct contradiction with the thermodynamic condition¬Ý(8) established in Sec.¬ÝII. The common mechanism in both proposals is an effective energy flux from the matter sector into Œõeff\Lambda_{\rm eff}, which increases the late-time expansion rate and reconciles the CMB-inferred and locally measured values of H0H_{0}. This mechanism is precisely characterized by ŒõÀôeff>0\dot{\Lambda}_{\rm eff}>0, or equivalently QÀô>0\dot{Q}>0. The conflict between thermodynamic admissibility and this specific mechanism of H0H_{0} resolution is not merely an accident of the specific forms of QQ chosen in those works. It is, in fact, a structural incompatibility that applies to the entire class of Œõ\LambdaCDM++diffusion models in UG with a pressureless matter fluid. We formalize this as the following result.

Theorem. In the Œõ\LambdaCDM++diffusion framework of UG described by Eqs.¬Ý(2)‚Äì(4), with a pressureless matter fluid (pm=0p_{m}=0), there exists no diffusion function Q‚Äã(t)Q(t) that simultaneously satisfies the second law of thermodynamics and produces a growing effective cosmological term, ŒõÀôeff=QÀô>0\dot{\Lambda}_{\rm eff}=\dot{Q}>0. Consequently, the specific mechanism of H0H_{0} tension resolution adopted in Refs.¬Ý[10, 11], namely, an energy flux from matter into Œõeff\Lambda_{\rm eff}, is thermodynamically inadmissible within this framework, regardless of the functional form chosen for Q‚Äã(t)Q(t).

Proof. From Eq.¬Ý(7), T‚ÄãSÀô=‚àía3‚ÄãQÀôT\dot{S}=-a^{3}\dot{Q}. Since T>0T>0 and a3>0a^{3}>0, the second law SÀô>0\dot{S}>0 requires QÀô<0\dot{Q}<0, i.e., QQ must decrease with cosmic time, which implies ŒõÀôeff=QÀô<0\dot{\Lambda}_{\rm eff}=\dot{Q}<0. The condition QÀô>0\dot{Q}>0 required to grow Œõeff\Lambda_{\rm eff} is therefore mutually exclusive with the second law, for any choice of Q‚Äã(t)Q(t). ‚ñ°\square

The physical content of this theorem is transparent. The second law demands that the matter fluid gains entropy, which requires energy to flow into the matter sector, i.e., QÀô<0\dot{Q}<0. But the H0H_{0} resolution mechanism of Refs.¬Ý[10, 11] requires energy to flow out of the matter sector and into Œõeff\Lambda_{\rm eff}, i.e., QÀô>0\dot{Q}>0. These two directions of energy flow are opposite and cannot be reconciled within this framework.

We emphasize that this result does not preclude alternative mechanisms for addressing the H0H_{0} tension within UG that do not rely on a growing Œõeff\Lambda_{\rm eff}. Such mechanisms, operating through different physical channels, may remain thermodynamically admissible and therefore constitute viable directions for future investigation. The present analysis establishes thermodynamic consistency as a necessary constraint that any future diffusion-based model in UG must satisfy alongside observational viability.
