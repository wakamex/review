Abstract

Abstract: We present a unified framework for the discussion of black hole thermodynamics of dd-dimensional static black holes with spherical, toroidal or compact hyperbolic horizon topology satisfying gt​t​gr​r=−1g_{tt}g_{rr}=-1 in Schwarzschild gauge. To that end, we consider any such black hole as a solution to an integrable 22-dimensional effective dilaton theory and thereby as a vacuum solution to an extended notion of dd-dimensional quasi-topological gravity. We show that the generating function determining f​(r)=−gt​tf(r)=-g_{tt} in the integrated equation of motion provides the thermodynamic mass in a generalised first law with entropy computed as the Wald entropy. The framework presented here can be applied to singular and regular black holes with flat or anti-de Sitter asymptotics.  

The Bekenstein-Hawking area law for the entropy of a black hole in general relativity (GR) Bekenstein (1973); Hawking (1975) is expected to receive corrections for beyond-GR black holes. These corrections can be accessed through Wald’s Noether charge formalism Wald (1993); Iyer and Wald (1994) — provided a given beyond-GR black hole arises as a known solution to a generally covariant gravitational theory. This is often not the case for phenomenological black hole models inspired by quantum gravity. 111See e.g. Bonanno and Reuter (2000, 2006); Falls et al. Final remarks—The entropy formula (46) establishes explicitly the corrections to the Bekenstein-Hawking area law for dd-dimensional static and asymptotically flat or anti-de Sitter beyond-GR black holes satisfying gt​t​gr​r=−1g_{tt}g_{rr}=-1 in Schwarzschild gauge when regarded as quasi-topological gravitational vacuum solutions. It will be insightful to supplement the derivation of the Wald entropy by a computation of the entropy based on Euclidean path integral methods Gibbons and Hawking (1977); Hawking (1978) to see whether these are equivalent or not Iyer and Wald (1995). Even though the boundary terms of the dd-dimensional theory are generally unknown, one could consider an analysis at the level of the 22-dimensional reduced theory using the boundary terms for Horndeski theory Padilla and Sivanesan (2012). For static black holes these would be a generalised Gibbons-Hawking-York term involving the non-minimal coupling h4h_{4} and supplemented by appropriate counterterms depending on the background asymptotics. It is overall fascinating to anticipate the depth of possibilities of addressing open questions in black hole physics Afshordi and others (2024); Buoninfante and others (2024); Carballo-Rubio and others (2025) based on generic 22-dimensional dilaton theory as reduced dd-dimensional gravity.
