The existence criterion of holomorphic discs for higher A∞A_{\infty} operations via minimal discs

Qiang Tan and Zuyi Zhang
Abstract

The main theorem of the paper provides an existence criterion of holomorphic discs for higher A∞A_{\infty} operations. The key step is to show that if a minimal disc in a Kähler manifold with boundary in a sequence of Lagrangian submanifolds intersecting transversely such that its partial Maslov indices are either all no less than 11 or all no larger than −1-1, then there is a holomorphic disc with the same image as this minimal disc. As a by-product, we show that all minimal discs in ℂ​Pm\mathbb{C}\mathrm{P}^{m} with boundary on ℝ​Pm\mathbb{R}\mathrm{P}^{m} are holomorphic.

1 Introduction

This paper is motivated by the A∞A_{\infty} category of symplectic manifolds arising from Lagrangian Floer theory. This category is equipped with a sequence of operations mlm_{l}, l=1,2,3,…l=1,2,3,\ldots, satisfying the A∞A_{\infty} relations [FOO+09], where the objects of this category are unobstructed Lagrangian submanifolds. These operations are defined by counting elements of zero-dimensional moduli spaces obtained by solving the Cauchy–Riemann equation. When l≥2l\geq 2, the moduli space consists of pseudo-holomorphic discs connecting intersection points of the given Lagrangians (Figure 1). And in this situation, the Maslov index of the discs is less or equal than −2-2. The main result of this paper gives a criterion for the existence of such discs on closed Kähler manifolds using partial Maslov indices.

Theorem 1.1.

Given a closed Kähler manifold (X,ω,J)(X,\omega,J) and a sequence of Lagrangians LkL_{k} with k=1,…,nk=1,\dots,n intersecting transversely in XX, suppose there is a smooth disc uu connecting x→=(x1,…,xn−1)\vec{x}=(x_{1},\ldots,x_{n-1}) and y∈L1∩Lny\in L_{1}\cap L_{n} with xk∈Lk∩Lk+1x_{k}\in L_{k}\cap L_{k+1} for k=1,…,n−1k=1,\ldots,n-1 ((Definition 2.6)). Then there is a minimal disc u0u_{0} connecting x→\vec{x} and yy homotopic to uu. If in addition, the partial Maslov indices ((Definition 4.5)) of u0u_{0} are either all no less than 11 or all no larger than −1-1, then there is a holomorphic map from 𝔻\mathbb{D} to XX with the same image as u0u_{0}.

Remark 1.2.

On Kähler surfaces (real dimension 4), there are two partial Maslov indices for u0u_{0}. Therefore the Maslov index of u0u_{0} is either greater than 2 or less than −2-2 in case the above theorem can be applied. It implies that the criterion only applies to ml,l≥2m_{l},\ l\geq 2, for Kähler surface. Similarly, given a Kähler manifold with real dimension 2​n2n, the criterion above is applicable for ml,l≥nm_{l},\ l\geq n. This is the reason why these holomorphic discs can correspond only to higher A∞A_{\infty} operations.

Oh [OH95] used the Brikhoff factorization to show that a Lagrangian subbundle of E|∂𝔻E|_{\partial\mathbb{D}} has a standard form that is characterized by the partial Maslov indices, where EE is a holomorphic vector bundle over the unit disc 𝔻\mathbb{D}. Unlike the Maslov index, partial Maslov indices are not topological and hard to compute. However, the positivity of the partial Maslov indices is equivalent to the Griffiths positivity (Definition 4.8) of the double of the pullback bundle through u0u_{0}.

Theorem 1.3.

Suppose LkL_{k} with k=1,…,nk=1,\dots,n is a sequence of Lagrangians in a Kähler manifold (Xm,ω,J)(X^{m},\omega,J) intersecting transversely. Let u:𝔻→Xu:\mathbb{D}\rightarrow X be a disc connecting the intersections x→,y\vec{x},y of {Lk}k\{L_{k}\}_{k}. Let EDE^{D} be the doubled holomorphic bundle defined as in Definition 4.7, where E=u∗​T1,0​XE=u^{*}T^{1,0}X and the boundary Lagrangian subbundle FkF_{k} is u∗​T1,0​Lk|u​(∂𝔻)∩Lku^{*}T^{1,0}L_{k}|_{u(\partial\mathbb{D})\cap L_{k}} ((Equation 4.1)) for k=1,…,mk=1,\ldots,m. Then the partial Maslov indices of uu are all larger than 11 if and only if EDE^{D} is Griffiths positive. ((The bundle EE is holomorphic by Lemma 2.12.))

As a by-product, we can describe all minimal discs in ℂ​Pm\mathbb{C}\mathrm{P}^{m} with boundary in ℝ​Pm\mathbb{R}\mathrm{P}^{m}. The proof is presented in Section 5

Corollary 1.4.

Suppose ℂ​Pm\mathbb{C}\mathrm{P}^{m} is equipped with the Fubini-Study metric. Then all minimal discs in ℂ​Pm\mathbb{C}\mathrm{P}^{m} with boundary in ℝ​Pm\mathbb{R}\mathrm{P}^{m} are all holomorphic. As a result, these discs are contained in a ℂ​P1⊂ℂ​Pm\mathbb{C}\mathrm{P}^{1}\subset\mathbb{C}\mathrm{P}^{m}.

The use of pseudo-holomorphic curves to study symplectic manifold was introduced by Gromov [GRO85]. Then Floer [FLO88] defined the Lagrangian Floer homology to solve a version of Arnold’s conjecture, where the differential of this homology is defined by counting the number of pseudo-holomorphic discs. Fukaya discovered the A∞A_{\infty} structure in Lagrangian Floer theory and it was later called the Fukaya category. However, Lagrangian Floer homology and Fukaya category may not be well defined in general because of the bubbling phenomena. Fukaya-Oh-Ohto-Ono [FOO+09] used algebraic methods to find out the obstructions to defining the A∞A_{\infty} structures. There has been extensive research on the Fukaya category. Abouzaid [ABO08] gives a thorough description of the Fukaya categroy on higher-genus surfaces. Hedden-Herald-Hogancamp-Kirk [HHH+20] calculate examples for the pillowcase. Cazassus provides a construction of equivariant Lagrangian Floer homology using the Morse model [CAZ24]. The second author gives a combinatorial way for computing the boundary maps in Lagrangian Floer theory when the symplectic manifold is a Cartesian product of closed surfaces [ZHA24, ZHA25]. The references on the Fukaya category include Auroux [AUR14] and Seidel [SEI08].

For another point of view, there are many studies on constructing pseudo-holomorphic curves. Gromov showed the existence of pseudo-holomorphic curves by analyzing the ∂¯\bar{\partial} operator directly [GRO85]. Siu-Yau [SY80], Micallef [MIC82], and Arezzo [ARE98] proved the existence of holomorphic curves using stable minimal surfaces. Recently, Chen [CHE25] tried to establish the existence of holomorphic curves in an open Kähler surface with its boundary on a given Lagrangian submanifold. This work is based on the existence of minimal surface of Ye [YE91].

We use the idea from Chen [CHE25] and Chen-Fraser [CF10]. Compared with [CHE25] [CF10], the ambient manifold in the present paper is a closed Kähler manifold. So the existence of the minimal surface is relatively straightforward. The boundary of the holomorphic curve in the current paper lies on nn Lagrangian submanifolds, where the nn-th unit roots are mapped to prescribed intersection points of these Lagrangians (Figure 1). The method used in [CHE25] [CF10] requires the kernel of the linearized Cauchy-Riemann operator to have dimension at least 2, and therefore imposes restrictions on the partial Maslov indices.

The arrangement of this paper is as follows. In Section 2, we provide the necessary background of symplectic geometry. Section 3 aims at proving the existence of minimal surface with the prescribed Lagrangian boundary condition. Section 4 defines the partial Maslov indices and Theorem 1.3 is proved. The proof of Theorem 1.1 and Corollary 1.4 is given in Section 5.

2 Preliminary

The goal of this section is to provide the necessary background on holomorphic discs in Kähler manifolds whose boundaries lie on given Lagrangian submanifolds. These Lagrangians are assumed to intersect transversely. After defining the Cauchy-Riemann operator, we give the definition of the Maslov index. For generic complex structures on Kähler manifolds, the moduli space of holomorphic discs (Definition 2.6) is generically a smooth manifold whose dimension can be computed from the Maslov index and the number of marked points (corresponding the unit roots in Definition 2.6) by the Riemann-Roch theorem [AUR14, Equation 2.5].

3 Minimal surface method

Then the exact argument in the previous can be applied since in this coordinate, the partial Maslov indices are all no less than 11 again. ∎

Proof of Corollary 1.4.

Let uu be a minimal discs in ℂ​Pm\mathbb{C}\mathrm{P}^{m} with boundary in ℝ​Pn\mathbb{R}\mathrm{P}^{n}. Suppose τ\tau is the anti-symplectic involution induced by taking the complex conjugation on ℂ​Pm\mathbb{C}\mathrm{P}^{m}. Then τ\tau maps uu to another disc such that the new disc and uu can be glued together to get a S2S^{2}. Then the pullback bundle E1E_{1} of T1,0​ℂ​Pm≅T​ℂ​PmT^{1,0}\mathbb{C}\mathrm{P}^{m}\cong T\mathbb{C}\mathrm{P}^{m} to this S2S^{2} is Griffiths positive since T​ℂ​PmT\mathbb{C}\mathrm{P}^{m} is Griffiths positive. Notice that the pullback of T(0,1)​ℂ​PmT^{(0,1)}\mathbb{C}\mathrm{P}^{m} through τ∘u:𝔻¯→ℂ​Pm\tau\circ u:\overline{\mathbb{D}}\rightarrow\mathbb{C}\mathrm{P}^{m} is the conjugate bundle of E:=u∗​T(0,1)​ℂ​PmE:=u^{*}T^{(0,1)}\mathbb{C}\mathrm{P}^{m}. Therefore, E1E_{1} is the doubled holomorphic bundle EDE^{D} ((Definition 4.7)). After applying Theorem 1.3 and Theorem 1.1, uu must be holomorphic. Thus the corollary follows. ∎
