Notes on the decomposition theorem for blowups

Hiroshi Iritani iritani@math.kyoto-u.ac.jp Department of Mathematics, Graduate School of Science, Kyoto University, Kitashirakawa-Oiwake-cho, Sakyo-ku, Kyoto, 606-8502, Japan
Abstract.

We discuss arithmetic and Hodge-theoretic properties of the isomorphisms appearing in the decomposition theorem [4] for quantum cohomology of blowups. These properties underpin the application to the rationality questions by Katzarkov-Kontsevich-Pantev-Yu [5].

Let XX be a smooth projective variety, and let Z⊂XZ\subset X be a smooth subvariety of codimension r≥2r\geq 2. Let X~{\widetilde{X}} be the blowup of XX along ZZ. The decomposition theorem [4, Theorems 1.1, 5.18] for blowups establishes the following isomorphism of (formal) quantum DD-modules:

(1) Ψ:QDM(X~)la≅τ∗QDM(X)la⊕⨁j=0r−2ςj∗QDM(Z)la\Psi\colon\operatorname{QDM}({\widetilde{X}})^{\rm la}\cong\tau^{*}\operatorname{QDM}(X)^{\rm la}\oplus\bigoplus_{j=0}^{r-2}\varsigma_{j}^{*}\operatorname{QDM}(Z)^{\rm la}

for some formal maps τ:H∗​(X~)→H∗​(X)\tau\colon H^{*}({\widetilde{X}})\to H^{*}(X) and ςj:H∗​(X~)→H∗​(Z)\varsigma_{j}\colon H^{*}({\widetilde{X}})\to H^{*}(Z) that induce a formal isomorphism at τ~=0{\tilde{\tau}}=0

(2) H∗​(X~)→H∗​(X)⊕H∗​(Z)⊕(r−1),τ~↦(τ​(τ~),{ςj​(τ~)}j=0r−2).H^{*}({\widetilde{X}})\to H^{*}(X)\oplus H^{*}(Z)^{\oplus(r-1)},\quad{\tilde{\tau}}\mapsto(\tau({\tilde{\tau}}),\{\varsigma_{j}({\tilde{\tau}})\}_{j=0}^{r-2}).

These maps Ψ\Psi, τ​(τ~)\tau({\tilde{\tau}}) and ςj​(τ~)\varsigma_{j}({\tilde{\tau}}) are defined over ℂ\mathbb{C} in [4]. In these notes we show the following:

The maps τ​(τ~)\tau({\tilde{\tau}}), ςj​(τ~)\varsigma_{j}({\tilde{\tau}}), and Ψ\Psi are essentially defined over a cyclotomic field. Furthermore, the change-of-variables map (2) restricts to a formal isomorphism between the subspaces of complexified Hodge classes. Remark 2.

All variables, as well as cohomology classes, carry both a ℤ\mathbb{Z}-degree and a ℤ/2​ℤ\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}-parity. Except for the parameters τi\tau^{i}, τ~i{\tilde{\tau}}^{i} and σi\sigma^{i} associated with odd cohomology classes, all other variables (QQ, zz, 𝔮1/𝔰\mathfrak{q}^{1/\mathfrak{s}}, etc.) have even parity. Note that the parity is not always congruent modulo 22 to the degree: we have deg⁡𝔮1/𝔰=1\deg\mathfrak{q}^{1/\mathfrak{s}}=1 when rr is odd, but the parity of 𝔮1/𝔰\mathfrak{q}^{1/\mathfrak{s}} remains even.

Corollary 11.

The formal map (τ,ς0,…,ςr−2):H∗​(X~)→H∗​(X)⊕H∗​(Z)⊕(r−1)(\tau,\varsigma_{0},\dots,\varsigma_{r-2})\colon H^{*}({\widetilde{X}})\to H^{*}(X)\oplus H^{*}(Z)^{\oplus(r-1)} restricts to a formal isomorphism between the subspaces of complexified Hodge classes. Remark 12.

By a similar argument, we can show that the formal maps τ:H∗​(X~)→H∗​(X)\tau\colon H^{*}({\widetilde{X}})\to H^{*}(X) and ςj:H∗​(X~)→H∗​(Z)\varsigma_{j}\colon H^{*}({\widetilde{X}})\to H^{*}(Z) preserve (complexified) algebraic classes, i.e. complex linear combinations of the Poincaré duals of algebraic cycles, and that the isomorphism Ψ|τ~\Psi|_{\tilde{\tau}} preserves algebraic classes whenever the parameter τ~{\tilde{\tau}} is algebraic. By the algebraic construction of virtual fundamental classes, we have a quantum product defined within Chow groups, and the fundamental solution MY​(t)M_{Y}(t) preserves algebraic classes whenever the parameter tt is algebraic.
