Relative Langlands duality and Koszul duality

Abstract.

Consider a pair of SS-dual hyperspherical varieties G↻XG\circlearrowright X and G∨↻X∨G^{\vee}\circlearrowright X^{\vee} equipped with equivariant quantizations Q​(X)Q(X), Q​(X∨)Q(X^{\vee}). Assume that the local conjecture of Ben-Zvi, Sakellaridis and Venkatesh holds for this pair, and also that X≃Tψ∗​(Y)X\simeq T^{*}_{\psi}(Y) is polarized, so that Q​(X)=Dψ​(Y)Q(X)=D_{\psi}(Y). Let B⊂GB\subset G (resp. B∨⊂G∨B^{\vee}\subset G^{\vee}) be Borel subgroups. Then using a variant of the S1S^{1}-equivariant localization of [BZN], we deduce an equivalence between the ℤ/2{\mathbb{Z}}/2-graded BB-equivariant category (Dψ(Y)−mod)ℤ/2)B(D_{\psi}(Y){\operatorname{-mod}})^{{\mathbb{Z}}/2})^{B} and the ℤ/2{\mathbb{Z}}/2-graded unipotent B∨B^{\vee}-monodromic category (Q​(X∨)​−modℤ/2)B∨,mon(Q(X^{\vee}){\operatorname{-mod}}^{{\mathbb{Z}}/2})^{B^{\vee},\operatorname{mon}}.

1. Introduction

1.1. Relative Langlands duality and equivalence of spherical categories

Let GG be a connected reductive group over ℂ{\mathbb{C}} and let 𝔜{\mathfrak{Y}} be a smooth affine spherical GG-variety – i.e. a smooth affine GG-variety with an open dense BB-orbit, where BB is a Borel subgroup of GG. We shall make an additional assumption: the stabilizer of a general point of 𝔜{\mathfrak{Y}} in BB is connected.

Remark 1.1.1.

In fact the setup of [BZSV] is more general. In loc.cit. the authors start with a hyper-spherical affine Poisson GG-variety 𝔛{\mathfrak{X}} such that the stabilizer of a general point in GG is connected. If one starts with a spherical variety 𝔜{\mathfrak{Y}} as above then one can attach to it a hyper-spherical variety 𝔛=T∗​𝔜{\mathfrak{X}}=T^{*}{\mathfrak{Y}}. Moreover, it is shown in [BZSV] that the stabilizer of a general point of 𝔛{\mathfrak{X}} in GG is connected if and only if the stabilizer of a general point of 𝔜{\mathfrak{Y}} in BB is connected. This language makes what follows much more symmetric, but the main results of this paper will be concerned with the cotangent case, i.e. the case when 𝔛=T∗​𝔜{\mathfrak{X}}=T^{*}{\mathfrak{Y}} as above, so we shall restrict ourselves to this setup from the very beginning.

One of the main conjectures of [BZSV] is that there should exist a dual hyper-spherical affine Poisson G∨G^{\vee}-variety 𝔛∨{\mathfrak{X}}^{\vee} endowed with the following structures:

1) A ℂ×\mathbb{C}^{\times}-action so that the Poisson structure has degree 2.

2) A graded quantization Qℏ​(𝔛∨)Q_{\hbar}({\mathfrak{X}}^{\vee}) which we are going to consider as a dg algebra with 0 differential.

Moreover, it is expected that the following holds. First for an algebraic group AA acting on a scheme WW we denote by D​(W)AD(W)^{A} the derived category of AA-equivariant DD-modules on WW and by D~​(W)A\widetilde{D}(W)^{A} the corresponding renormalized equivariant derived category – i.e. the cocompletion of the category consisting of objects of the usual equivariant derived category which have coherent cohomology in finitely many degrees (i.e. the category of locally compact objects in D​(W/A)D(W/A)); the difference between the renormalized equivariant derived category and the usual equivariant derived category is discussed e.g. in [DG]). Typically we are going to deal with scheme WW where AA has finitely many orbits (or and ind-scheme which is an a colimit of such schemes), so in that case we can work with the corresponding derived category of equivariant constructible sheaves.

Conjecture 1.1.2.

The category D~​(𝔜​(𝒦))G​(𝒪)⋊ℂ×\widetilde{D}({\mathfrak{Y}}({\mathcal{K}}))^{G({\mathcal{O}})\rtimes\mathbb{C}^{\times}} is equivalent to (Qℏ​(𝔛∨)​−mod)G∨(Q_{\hbar}({\mathfrak{X}}^{\vee}){\operatorname{-mod}})^{G^{\vee}} (the derived category of dg-modules over the corresponding dg-algebra endowed with a compatible action of G∨G^{\vee}). Here ℂ×\mathbb{C}^{\times} on the left is the “loop rotation”, and the corresponding equivariant parameter corresponds to ℏ\hbar on the right.

In fact, the main Conjecture of [BZSV] is more general: instead of starting with a spherical variety 𝔜{\mathfrak{Y}} one can start with a hyperspherical GG-variety 𝔛{\mathfrak{X}} (in the above situation one can take 𝔛=T∗​𝔜{\mathfrak{X}}=T^{*}{\mathfrak{Y}}). This makes the story more symmetric, but also more complicated. We shall refer to the situation when 𝔛=T∗​𝔜{\mathfrak{X}}=T^{*}{\mathfrak{Y}} as the cotangent case. In this paper we shall be mostly concerned with that situation or with the so called twisted cotangent case – cf. §1.5.

1.2. Koszul duality conjecture

In addition to Conjecture 1.1.2 the following conjecture was formulated in [FGT]. First let RR be a (non-commutative) ring endowed with a strong action of an algebraic group BB; by definition this means that BB acts on RR and there is a homomorphism of Lie algebras ι:𝔟→R\iota\colon\mathfrak{b}\to R so that the derivative of the BB-action is equal to the adjoint action of ι\iota. Then one can talk about the category of monodromic RR-modules with unipotent monodromy. By definition this is the category of modules MM over RR endowed with a compatible action of BB so that difference between the derivative of this action and the action of 𝔟\mathfrak{b} on MM coming from ι\iota is equal to the derivative of some fixed character λ:B→𝔾m\lambda\colon B\to{\mathbb{G}}_{m} (it is easy to see that this definition does not depend on the choice of λ\lambda).

Conjecture 1.2.1.

Let BB denote a Borel subroup of GG, and let B∨B^{\vee} be a Borel subgroup of G∨G^{\vee}. Let (Qℏ=1​(𝔛∨)​−mod)Bmon∨(Q_{\hbar=1}({\mathfrak{X}}^{\vee}){\operatorname{-mod}})^{B^{\vee}_{\operatorname{mon}}} denote the derived category of B∨B^{\vee}-monodromic modules over the (non-graded) algebra Qℏ=1​(𝔛∨)Q_{\hbar=1}({\mathfrak{X}}^{\vee}) with unipotent monodromy. Then the categories D​(𝔜)BD({\mathfrak{Y}})^{B} and (Qℏ=1​(𝔛∨)​−mod)Bmon∨(Q_{\hbar=1}({\mathfrak{X}}^{\vee}){\operatorname{-mod}})^{B^{\vee}_{\operatorname{mon}}} are Koszul dual. Similarly, the category D​(𝔜)BmonD({\mathfrak{Y}})^{B_{\operatorname{mon}}} is Koszul dual to (Qℏ=1​(𝔛∨)​−mod)B∨(Q_{\hbar=1}({\mathfrak{X}}^{\vee}){\operatorname{-mod}})^{B^{\vee}}.

Note that Koszul duality in particular means that the two categories above should be endowed with some additional gradings (and the duality is essentially an equivalence of bigraded categories which mixes the gradings in some particular way). To avoid this issue one can pass to the world of ℤ/2{\mathbb{Z}}/2-graded categories. For a ℤ\mathbb{Z}-graded category DD we shall denote by Dℤ/2D^{{\mathbb{Z}}/2} the corresponding ℤ/2{\mathbb{Z}}/2-graded category. Then we get the following version of Conjecture 1.2.1:

Conjecture 1.2.2.

The BB-equivariant category (D​(𝔜)ℤ/2)B(D({\mathfrak{Y}})^{\mathbb{Z}/2})^{B} is equivalent to the B∨B^{\vee}-unipotent monodromic category (Qℏ=1​(𝔛∨)​−modℤ/2)Bmon∨(Q_{\hbar=1}({\mathfrak{X}}^{\vee}){\operatorname{-mod}}^{\mathbb{Z}/2})^{B^{\vee}_{\operatorname{mon}}}.111Completions of ℤ/2\mathbb{Z}/2-graded categories are briefly discussed in §2.2. Similarly, the category (D​(𝔜)ℤ/2)Bmon(D({\mathfrak{Y}})^{\mathbb{Z}/2})^{B_{\operatorname{mon}}} is equivalent to (Qℏ=1​(𝔛∨)​−modℤ/2)B∨(Q_{\hbar=1}({\mathfrak{X}}^{\vee}){\operatorname{-mod}}^{\mathbb{Z}/2})^{B^{\vee}}.

1.3. What is done in this paper

The purpose of this paper is to do the following:

1) Develop a general framework for proving Conjecture 1.2.2. This general formalism will show that Conjecture 1.2.2 follows from Conjecture 1.1.2 up to some explicit statement.

2) Check that the above explicit statement is satisfied in a number of examples (in which Conjecture 1.1.2 is known).

Let us explain the above points in more detail. What we are actually going to prove is this:

Theorem 1.3.1.
  1. (1)

    Assume the validity of Conjecture 1.1.2 for given 𝔜{\mathfrak{Y}} and 𝔛∨{\mathfrak{X}}^{\vee}. Then the category (D​(𝔜)ℤ/2)B(D({\mathfrak{Y}})^{\mathbb{Z}/2})^{B} is equivalent to (Qℏ=1​(𝔛∨)​−modℤ/2)Bmon∨(Q_{\hbar=1}({\mathfrak{X}}^{\vee}){\operatorname{-mod}}^{\mathbb{Z}/2})^{B^{\vee}_{\operatorname{mon}}}.

  2. (2)

    Similar results hold when (D​(𝔜)ℤ/2)B(D({\mathfrak{Y}})^{\mathbb{Z}/2})^{B} is replaced by (D​(𝔜)ℤ/2)Bmon(D({\mathfrak{Y}})^{\mathbb{Z}/2})^{B_{\operatorname{mon}}}, and (Qℏ=1​(𝔛∨)​−modℤ/2)Bmon∨(Q_{\hbar=1}({\mathfrak{X}}^{\vee}){\operatorname{-mod}}^{\mathbb{Z}/2})^{B^{\vee}_{\operatorname{mon}}} is replaced by (Qℏ=1​(𝔛∨)​−modℤ/2)B∨(Q_{\hbar=1}({\mathfrak{X}}^{\vee}){\operatorname{-mod}}^{\mathbb{Z}/2})^{B^{\vee}}.

1.8. Idea of the proof

Let us very briefly outline the basic idea of the proof. We start with the equivalence of Conjecture 1.1.2 and choose a regular coweight λ∈𝔱\lambda\in{\mathfrak{t}} of GG. (Here 𝔱=Lie⁡𝖳{\mathfrak{t}}=\operatorname{Lie}{\mathsf{T}} is a Cartan Lie subalgebra in 𝔤=Lie⁡G{\mathfrak{g}}=\operatorname{Lie}G.) After passing to the corresponding ℤ/2\mathbb{Z}/2-graded categories everything becomes linear over Sym⁡(𝔱∗⊕ℂ)\operatorname{Sym}({\mathfrak{t}}^{*}\oplus{\mathbb{C}}) and we can perform the following procedures to both sides of the equivalence of Conjecture 1.1.2:

1) Completion at a=(λ,1)a=(\lambda,1).

2) Restriction to the point 1 in the second summand of 𝔞=𝔱⊕ℂ\mathfrak{a}={\mathfrak{t}}\oplus{\mathbb{C}}. We get a category linear over Sym⁡(𝔱∗)\operatorname{Sym}({\mathfrak{t}}^{*})).

3) Shift by λ\lambda in 𝔱{\mathfrak{t}} (i.e. this is just the identity operation on the level of abstract categories, but we change the action of Sym⁡(𝔱∗)\operatorname{Sym}({\mathfrak{t}}^{*})).

We then check that the above procedures applies to the LHS and the RHS of the ℤ/2\mathbb{Z}/2-version of the equivalence of Conjecture 1.1.2 lead precisely to the LHS and the RHS of the equivalence of Theorem 1.3.1. The other statements announced above are proven by similar techniques.

One remark is in order: a priori this construction depends on the choice of λ\lambda. However, we show in §4.5 that the resulting equivalence is in fact independent of this choice.

We should note that our approach is very similar to the arguments of [BZN], although with some differences.

2. Generalities on completions of equivariant derived categories

2.1. Completion of a dg-category

Let 𝒞\mathcal{C} be a cocomplete dg category which is linear over a commutative regular noetherian ring RR (i.e. it is enriched over the corresponding dg category of RR-modules). For any x∈Spec(R)x\in\mathop{\operatorname{\rm Spec}}(R) let R^x\widehat{R}_{x} be the completion of RR at xx. We consider the category R​−modx∧R{\operatorname{-mod}}^{\wedge}_{x} which can be described as the cocompletion of the category of coherent objects of RR-mod, which are set-theoretically supported at the closure of xx (usually we will be applying this to a closed point xx). We then define 𝒞x∧=𝒞​⊗R​−mod​R​−modx∧\mathcal{C}^{\wedge}_{x}=\mathcal{C}\underset{R{\operatorname{-mod}}}{\otimes}R{\operatorname{-mod}}^{\wedge}_{x}. We have a natural functor from 𝒞\mathcal{C} to 𝒞x∧\mathcal{C}^{\wedge}_{x}. If in addition we assume that 𝒞\mathcal{C} is compactly generated and that Hom’s between compact objects are compact objects of RR-mod (which in our case is the same as coherent complexes), then for two compact objects XX and YY the space of Hom’s between their images in 𝒞x∧\mathcal{C}^{\wedge}_{x} is equal to Hom(X,Y)​⊗𝑅​R^x{\mathop{\operatorname{\rm Hom}}}(X,Y)\underset{R}{\otimes}\widehat{R}_{x} (warning: the image of compact objects in the completion is usually not compact).

Similar (somewhat easier) constructions work for the fiber 𝒞x\mathcal{C}_{x} of 𝒞\mathcal{C} at xx. Also the constructions can be carried over in the world of differential ℤ/2\mathbb{Z}/2-graded categories instead of dg categories.

3.2. Proof of Theorem 3.1.3

To prove Theorem 3.1.3 we need two preparatory lemmas. Let 𝔜{\mathfrak{Y}} be a spherical GG-variety, and let 𝔛=Tψ∗​𝔜{\mathfrak{X}}=T^{*}_{\psi}{\mathfrak{Y}} be its twisted cotangent bundle. If 𝔛{\mathfrak{X}} is hyperspherical [BZSV, §3.5.1], then according to [BZSV, Proposition 3.7.4.(b)], the BB-stabilizers of points in the open BB-orbit in 𝔜{\mathfrak{Y}} are connected.

Lemma 3.2.1 (D. Timashev).

If the BB-stabilizers in the open BB-orbit in a spherical GG-variety 𝔜{\mathfrak{Y}} are connected, then the BB-stabilizer of any point y∈𝔜y\in{\mathfrak{Y}} is connected.

Proof.

First we consider the closure 𝔜′{\mathfrak{Y}}^{\prime} of a GG-orbit in 𝔜{\mathfrak{Y}}, and assume that the BB-stabilizer of a general point in 𝔜′{\mathfrak{Y}}^{\prime} is connected. Then it follows that the BB-stabilizer of any point y′∈𝔜′y^{\prime}\in{\mathfrak{Y}}^{\prime} is connected. Indeed, according to [K2, Corollary 3.3], the group of connected components StabB⁡(y′)/StabB0⁡(y′)\operatorname{Stab}_{B}(y^{\prime})/\operatorname{Stab}_{B}^{0}(y^{\prime}) is a subquotient of the similar group StabB⁡(y′′)/StabB0⁡(y′′)\operatorname{Stab}_{B}(y^{\prime\prime})/\operatorname{Stab}_{B}^{0}(y^{\prime\prime}) for y′′∈𝔜′y^{\prime\prime}\in{\mathfrak{Y}}^{\prime} lying in a bigger BB-orbit. By induction in the set of BB-orbits in 𝔜′{\mathfrak{Y}}^{\prime} with its adjacency order, we see that StabB⁡(y′)/StabB0⁡(y′)\operatorname{Stab}_{B}(y^{\prime})/\operatorname{Stab}_{B}^{0}(y^{\prime}) is a subquotient of StabB⁡(y′′)/StabB0⁡(y′′)\operatorname{Stab}_{B}(y^{\prime\prime})/\operatorname{Stab}_{B}^{0}(y^{\prime\prime}) for y′′y^{\prime\prime} in the open BB-orbit in 𝔜′{\mathfrak{Y}}^{\prime}. But the latter group is trivial by our assumption.

It remains to study the BB-stabilizers of points in BB-orbits open in their GG-orbits in 𝔜{\mathfrak{Y}}. For a point yy in such a BB-orbit, the group StabB⁡(y)/StabB0⁡(y)\operatorname{Stab}_{B}(y)/\operatorname{Stab}_{B}^{0}(y) is trivial iff the lattice Λ𝔜′\Lambda_{{\mathfrak{Y}}^{\prime}} is saturated in Λ\Lambda. Here Λ\Lambda is the lattice of weights of BB (i.e. Λ=X∗​(𝖳)\Lambda=X^{*}({\mathsf{T}})), and Λ𝔜′\Lambda_{{\mathfrak{Y}}^{\prime}} is the lattice of weights of BB-semi-invariant rational functions on the GG-orbit 𝔜′=G.y{\mathfrak{Y}}^{\prime}=G.y. According to [K1, Theorem 1.3.(b)], any BB-semi-invariant rational fuction on 𝔜′{\mathfrak{Y}}^{\prime} extends to a BB-semi-invariant rational function on the whole of 𝔜{\mathfrak{Y}}. Clearly, the BB-weights of the function and its extension coincide. The resulting rational functions on 𝔜{\mathfrak{Y}} can be characterized as BB-semi-invariant rational functions without poles or zeros at the BB-invariant irreducible divisors in 𝔜{\mathfrak{Y}} containing 𝔜′{\mathfrak{Y}}^{\prime}. It follows that Λ𝔜′\Lambda_{{\mathfrak{Y}}^{\prime}} is saturated in the lattice of BB-weights of BB-semi-invariant rational functions on 𝔜{\mathfrak{Y}}. Hence the triviality of StabB⁡(y)/StabB0⁡(y)\operatorname{Stab}_{B}(y)/\operatorname{Stab}_{B}^{0}(y) for general y∈𝔜′y\in{\mathfrak{Y}}^{\prime} follows from the triviality of StabB⁡(y)/StabB0⁡(y)\operatorname{Stab}_{B}(y)/\operatorname{Stab}_{B}^{0}(y) for general y∈𝔜y\in{\mathfrak{Y}}.

The lemma is proved. ∎

3.3. Proof of Theorem 1.7.1

The proof follows the same pattern as the proof of Theorem 1.3.1: we still apply the same procedures 1, 2 above for a=(λ,1)a=(\lambda,1). The following is not formally an example to which the results of the present paper are directly applicable. However, we hope that some upgrade of those results will also prove an analogue of Theorem 1.3.1 in this case.

Then we still get an isomorphism between the Grothendieck group of the category of ψ\psi-twisted BB-equivariant DD-modules on 𝔜{\mathfrak{Y}} (which are the same as B×(𝔾a,χ))B\times(\mathbb{G}_{a},\chi))-equivariant DD-modules on ψ\psi where χ\chi denotes the identity homomorphism 𝔾a→𝔾a\mathbb{G}_{a}\to\mathbb{G}_{a}) – we just need to say that the group generated by conormal bundles to the relevant G×𝔾aG\times\mathbb{G}_{a}-orbits in T∗​ψ×T∗​ℬT^{*}\psi\times T^{*}\mathcal{B} maps isomorphically onto the group generated by components of Λ~\widetilde{\Lambda} under the map which sends every cycle to its intersection with the preimage of 1 under the moment map with respect to 𝔾a\mathbb{G}_{a} followed by projection to 𝔛×T∗​ℬ{\mathfrak{X}}\times T^{*}\mathcal{B}. This proves Conjecture 1.6.1 in the case of §3.4.3.

In fact, we can relax the above requirements a little further (so called pseudospherical case). Namely, it is enough to require that 𝔛{\mathfrak{X}} is as above, and 𝔛∨=Tψ∨∗​𝔜∨{\mathfrak{X}}^{\vee}=T^{*}_{\psi^{\vee}}{\mathfrak{Y}}^{\vee} where 𝔜∨{\mathfrak{Y}}^{\vee} is just a B∨B^{\vee}-variety and ψ∨\psi^{\vee} is a B∨B^{\vee}-equivariant torsor on 𝔜∨{\mathfrak{Y}}^{\vee}, provided that

a) The quantization Qℏ=1​(𝔛∨)Q_{\hbar=1}({\mathfrak{X}}^{\vee}) is given by the quantum hamiltonian reduction of the algebra of differential operators on ψ∨\psi^{\vee} with respect to 𝔾a\mathbb{G}_{a}.

b) The assertion of Lemma 3.2.1 holds in this case (note that we can not formally apply Lemma 3.2.1 here, since 𝔜∨{\mathfrak{Y}}^{\vee} in this case is not a G∨G^{\vee}-variety).

These requirements are satisfied in the case of §3.4.4. Namely, recall that G∨=SO(2​n+2)×Sp(2​n)G^{\vee}={\mathop{\operatorname{\rm SO}}}(2n+2)\times{\mathop{\operatorname{\rm Sp}}}(2n) or SO(2​n)×Sp(2​n){\mathop{\operatorname{\rm SO}}}(2n)\times{\mathop{\operatorname{\rm Sp}}}(2n). We consider the second case, the first one being absolutely similar. We choose a Lagrangian subspace L⊂ℂ−2​nL\subset{\mathbb{C}}^{2n}_{-} invariant with respect to the Borel subgroup B∨⊂G∨B^{\vee}\subset G^{\vee}. We set L∗=ℂ−2​n/LL^{*}={\mathbb{C}}^{2n}_{-}/L, and 𝔜∨=ℂ+2​n⊗L∗{\mathfrak{Y}}^{\vee}={\mathbb{C}}^{2n}_{+}\otimes L^{*}. Finally, ψ∨\psi^{\vee} is the descent of the Heisenberg extension 0→𝔾a→Heis→ℂ+2​n⊗ℂ−2​n→00\to{\mathbb{G}}_{a}\to\operatorname{Heis}\to{\mathbb{C}}^{2n}_{+}\otimes{\mathbb{C}}^{2n}_{-}\to 0 to 𝔜∨{\mathfrak{Y}}^{\vee}.

We have a natural homomorphism (characteristic cycle) from K​((D​(𝔜∨)ℤ/2)loc.compB∨,ψ∨)K((D({\mathfrak{Y}}^{\vee})^{{\mathbb{Z}}/2})^{B^{\vee},\psi^{\vee}}_{\operatorname{loc.comp}}) to H​(Λ∨)H(\Lambda^{\vee}) (from the Grothendieck group of the category of locally compact objects to the top Borel-Moore homology). According to [FGT, Remark 4.3.2], a basis in the latter group is formed by the fundamental cycles of twisted conormal bundles of relevant B∨B^{\vee}-orbits in 𝔜∨{\mathfrak{Y}}^{\vee}. It follows that the above homomorphism of KK-groups is surjective. However,

dimH​(Λ∨)=♯​Irr⁡Λ∨=♯​Irr⁡Λ=dimH​(Λ)=dimK​((D​(𝔜)ℤ/2)compBmon,ψ)=dimK​((D​(𝔜∨)ℤ/2)compB∨,ψ∨)=dimK​((D​(𝔜∨)ℤ/2)loc.compB∨,ψ∨).\dim H(\Lambda^{\vee})=\sharp\operatorname{Irr}\Lambda^{\vee}=\sharp\operatorname{Irr}\Lambda=\dim H(\Lambda)=\dim K((D({\mathfrak{Y}})^{{\mathbb{Z}}/2})^{B_{\operatorname{mon}},\psi}_{\operatorname{comp}})\\ =\dim K((D({\mathfrak{Y}}^{\vee})^{{\mathbb{Z}}/2})^{B^{\vee},\psi^{\vee}}_{\operatorname{comp}})=\dim K((D({\mathfrak{Y}}^{\vee})^{{\mathbb{Z}}/2})^{B^{\vee},\psi^{\vee}}_{\operatorname{loc.comp}}).

Here the second equality is [FGT, Proposition 5.1.1], the fourth equality follows from Lemma 3.2.1, the fifth equality follows from Theorem 1.3.1 combined with [BFT1, Theorem 2.2.1], and the last equality follows from the duality discussed in the beginning of this section. We conclude that the surjective homomorphism K​((D​(𝔜∨)ℤ/2)loc.compB∨,ψ∨)↠H​(Λ∨)K((D({\mathfrak{Y}}^{\vee})^{{\mathbb{Z}}/2})^{B^{\vee},\psi^{\vee}}_{\operatorname{loc.comp}})\twoheadrightarrow H(\Lambda^{\vee}) must be an isomorphism. (In particular, the stabilizers in B∨B^{\vee} of points in relevant orbits in 𝔜∨{\mathfrak{Y}}^{\vee} are all connected.) All in all, we obtain an isomorphism of WW-modules in Conjecture 1.6.1.
