Fillable structures on negative-definite
Seifert fibred spaces

Alberto Cavallo and Irena Matkovič HUN-REN Alfréd Rényi Insitute of Mathematics, Budapest 1053, Hungary acavallo@impan.pl Uppsala Universitet, Uppsala 751 06, Sweden irma6504@student.uu.se
Abstract.

We classify fillable contact structures on all negative-definite star-shaped plumbings. Along the way, we show that such Seifert fibred spaces admit a unique negative maximal twisting number, and compute it explicitly using the Alexander filtration in lattice cohomology. In particular, we show that the negative-twisting tight structures on these manifolds are induced by the Stein structures on the minimal resolution of the underlying complex surface singularity. As an application, we provide a necessary condition for a negative-definite Seifert fibred space to admit a separating contact-type embedding in a strong symplectic filling of a generalised LL-space.

1. Introduction

Investigating contact structures on Seifert fibred spaces provides a bridge between 3-dimensional topology and algebraic geometry. Indeed, links of normal surface singularities naturally inherit both Seifert fibred and contact structure. In this paper, we adopt a topological perspective and classify all the symplectically fillable contact structures on negative-definite star-shaped plumbings; a special case of which are canonically oriented Brieskorn spheres.

The object of our study then consists of Seifert fibred spaces with base orbifold homeomorphic to S2S^{2}. A 3-manifold of this kind, denoted as M=M​(e0;r1,…,rn)M=M(e_{0};r_{1},\dots,r_{n}) with the integral Euler number e0∈ℤe_{0}\in\mathbb{Z}, and nn singular fibres with parameters r1,…,rn∈(0,1)∩ℚr_{1},\dots,r_{n}\in(0,1)\cap\mathbb{Q}, can be represented with a star-shaped plumbing graph Γ\Gamma which we call the standard graph of MM, as shown in Figure 1. In these diagrams the Euler number e0e_{0} is the framing of the central vertex, while the parameter rir_{i} determines the framings on the ii-th leg via the negative continued fraction expansion of −1ri-\frac{1}{r_{i}}. The construction is explained in full detail in [Saveliev]. Note that when n⩾3n\geqslant 3 the 3-manifold MM uniquely determines the graph Γ\Gamma.

We also set the convention that every time we consider a 3-manifold presented by a graph, we assume that it inherits the orientation consistent with it. Hence, abusing the notation, we call the Seifert fibred space negative-definite if and only if e​(M):=e0+r1+⋯+rn<0e(M):=e_{0}+r_{1}+\cdots+r_{n}<0.

Brieskorn spheres are Seifert fibred homology spheres. They are the complete intersection singularities, described by exponent parameters ai⩾2a_{i}\geqslant 2, such that gcd⁡(ai,aj)=1\gcd(a_{i},a_{j})=1 for i≠ji\not=j, as Σ​(a1,…,an)\Sigma(a_{1},...,a_{n}), see [Saveliev, Section 1] for the exact definition. Note that the Brieskorn parameters (a1,…,an)(a_{1},...,a_{n}) are related to the Seifert parameters (e0;r1,…,rn)(e_{0};r_{1},...,r_{n}) via the equations:

(1.1) r1+…+rn=−e0−1a1​⋯​an,ri=biai(bi∈ℤ>0),r_{1}+...+r_{n}=-e_{0}-\dfrac{1}{a_{1}\cdots a_{n}},\ \ \ \ \ \ r_{i}=\dfrac{b_{i}}{a_{i}}\ \ \ (b_{i}\in\mathbb{Z}_{>0})\>,

and that the standard graph Γ\Gamma of Σ​(a1,…,an)\Sigma(a_{1},...,a_{n}) is always negative-definite. For basic definitions, and results regarding plumbing calculus we refer to [GS, Saveliev].

Classification of negative-twisting tight structures

We recall that the main invariant in the classification of tight contact structures on Seifert fibred spaces is the maximal twisting number, which captures the maximal difference between the contact framing and the fibration framing of (any Legendrian realisation of) the regular fibre KK of MM. On negative-definite Seifert fibred spaces we, first, find surgery presentations for all its negative-twisting tight structures, and then show that all the fillable structures are such. The exact number of structures can be computed directly from the Seifert coefficients as shown in the proof of Proposition LABEL:prop:previous.

Theorem 1.1.

Suppose that M=M​(e0;r1,…,rn)M=M(e_{0};r_{1},\dots,r_{n}) has negative-definite standard graph. The negative-twisting tight contact structures on MM are precisely the Legendrian surgeries on all possible Legendrian realisations of the complete blow-down of the standard graph; moreover, they are all Stein fillable and distinguished, up to isotopy, by their contact invariant c+c^{+} in H​F+​(−M)HF^{+}(-M).

Furthermore, every structure ξ\xi on MM with non-vanishing c+​(ξ)c^{+}(\xi) is negative-twisting, and then every symplectically fillable structure on MM is as above.

Out of classification we read that (in our case) all negative-twisting tight structures are (Stein) fillable. Furthermore, it is possible to show that when e0<−1e_{0}<-1 and n=2,3n=2,3 our theorem actually covers every possible tight contact structure. Note that when c+c^{+} is non-vanishing the contact structure has no half convex Giroux torsion, because otherwise it would be zero-twisting; this means that for negative-definite Seifert fibred spaces half convex torsion obstructs fillability.

Note that for Seifert fibred spaces with e0⩽−ne_{0}\leqslant-n, which are all LL-spaces, such a classification result has generally been expected; in fact, with such a restriction the classification was previously established for n=3,4,n=3,4, and it has been in the case of n=3n=3 extended also to e0=−2e_{0}=-2 with negative-definite standard graph [Wu, Shah, Medetogullari, G-, Tosun]. On the contrary, for e0=−1e_{0}=-1 the uniqueness of the twisting number, when MM has negative-definite standard graph, takes us by surprise; in fact, in this case the only classification previously in the literature is for M​(−1;12,13,k6​k+1)M(-1;\frac{1}{2},\frac{1}{3},\frac{k}{6k+1}) in [MT2], extended to negative surgeries on positive torus knots in an unpublished note by Tosun111To date, the note only contains a proof that every fillable structure on a negative-definite M​(−1;r1,r2,r3)M(-1;r_{1},r_{2},r_{3}) is negative-twisting, and if M≃S−r3​(Tp,q)M\simeq S^{3}_{-r}(T_{p,q}) for r∈ℚ>0r\in\mathbb{Q}_{>0} then it is obtained as described in Theorem 1.1. The techniques used by Tosun strictly depend on the explicit values of the Seifert coefficients of MM.. Note that LL-spaces with e0=−1e_{0}=-1, for which the complete classification is known [Irena, GLS], are never negative-definite; all tight contact structures on these manifolds are actually zero-twisting.

Since (almost) every surgery on a torus link is a Seifert fibred space, Theorem 1.1 immediately implies the following corollary. We assume 1⩽p⩽q1\leqslant p\leqslant q coprime and k⩾1k\geqslant 1.

Corollary 1.2.

Our classification holds for every surgery on a positive (resp. negative) torus link Tk​p,±k​qT_{kp,\pm kq} whose surgery matrix Λ\Lambda is negative-definite (resp. has Λi​i>−p​q\Lambda_{ii}>-pq and det(Λ)<0\det(\Lambda)<0).

Obstructing contact type-embeddings of Brieskorn spheres

Using the classification in Theorem 1.1 we can guarantee that a ξ\xi with self-conjugate contact invariant is itself self-conjugate; that is, ξ\xi is contact isotopic to its conjugate ξ¯\overline{\xi}. Our obstruction then relies on proving Proposition 1.5, which shows that if ξ\xi is a self-conjugate structure among the ones in Theorem 1.1 then (M,𝔰ξ)(M,\mathfrak{s}_{\xi}) cannot have vanishing correction term, and this puts a strong restriction on the structure of the Heegaard Floer groups of the 3-manifold we are considering.

Proposition 1.5.

Suppose that MM is a negative-definite Seifert fibred space different from S3S^{3}, and let 𝔰0\mathfrak{s}_{0} be a spin structure on MM. Then every fillable contact structure ξ\xi on MM such that 𝔰ξ=𝔰0\mathfrak{s}_{\xi}=\mathfrak{s}_{0} has either c+​(ξ)≠Θ𝔰0+c^{+}(\xi)\neq\Theta^{+}_{\mathfrak{s}_{0}} or the Heegaard Floer correction term d:=d​(M,𝔰0)d:=d(M,\mathfrak{s}_{0}) is positive.

We recall that Θ𝔰0+∈H​F+​(−M,𝔰0)\Theta^{+}_{\mathfrak{s}_{0}}\in HF^{+}(-M,\mathfrak{s}_{0}) is the only non-zero element in Ker⁡U∩Un⋅H​F+​(−M,𝔰0)\operatorname{Ker}U\>\cap U^{n}\cdot HF^{+}(-M,\mathfrak{s}_{0}) for every n⩾0n\geqslant 0. A weaker version of Proposition 1.5 was given by Karakurt in [Karakurt, Theorem 1.4] as an obstruction to the planarity of ξ\xi.

We say that a contact 3-manifold (Y,ξ)(Y,\xi) admits a separating contact-type embedding in a symplectic filling (X,ω)(X,\omega) when ω|ν​(Y)\omega\lvert_{\nu(Y)} coincides with the symplectisation of (Y,ξ)(Y,\xi), and YY disconnects XX into two pieces none of them being a symplectic cap of (Y,ξ)(Y,\xi). In [MT, MT3] Mark and Tosun study the problem of whether a Brieskorn sphere admits a separating contact-type embedding in a symplectic 4-manifold333The proof of [MT, Theorem 4.6] has a gap. The argument is based on the existence of a singular fibre with particular contact behaviour, claimed in [MT, Proposition 4.5]. However, in [MT, Equation (16)] of its proof the relation between the Seifert constants of a Brieskorn sphere is miscited. To conclude the proof in the same way using the corrected equation, the Brieskorn coefficients would at least need to fulfil the inequality a1​⋯​an−1<ana_{1}\cdots a_{n-1}<a_{n}. This leaves many examples, like Σ​(3,4,5)\Sigma(3,4,5) and Σ​(2,5,7)\Sigma(2,5,7), untreated.. We then obtain the following result by applying Proposition 1.5, together with the obstruction from [MT, Theorem 2.1]. This now provides a complete proof of [MT, Theorem 1.1].

Corollary 1.6.

No canonically oriented Brieskorn sphere Σ​(a1,…,an)\Sigma(a_{1},...,a_{n}) admits a separating contact-type embedding in any symplectic filling of a lens space. In particular, they do not arise as the boundary of any rationally convex domain in ℂ2\mathbb{C}^{2}.

Let 𝔽\mathbb{F} be the field with two elements. In the next statement we use the original definition of LL-space given by Ozsváth and Szabó; namely, we say that a 3-manifold YY is a generalised LL-space if H​Fred​(Y)=0HF_{\text{red}}(Y)=0, or equivalently that the dimension of H​F^​(Y,𝔰)\widehat{HF}(Y,\mathfrak{s}) as an 𝔽\mathbb{F}-vector space is one if 𝔰\mathfrak{s} is torsion, and zero otherwise.

Theorem 1.7.

Suppose that MM is a negative-definite Seifert fibred space. If (M,ξ)(M,\xi) admits a separating contact-type embedding in a strong symplectic filling (X,ω)(X,\omega) of a generalised LL-space, then dim𝔽H​F^d​(M,𝔰ξ)=1\dim_{\mathbb{F}}\widehat{HF}_{d}(M,\mathfrak{s}_{\xi})=1. In particular, this happens when (M,ξ)(M,\xi) is the boundary of a rationally convex domain in a closed Kälher four-manifold (X^,ω,J)(\widehat{X},\omega,J).

Theorem 1.7 holds for example in the case of X^=ℂ​P2\widehat{X}=\mathbb{C}P^{2} with its unique symplectic structure. This gives evidence for a positive answer to [MT3, Question 1.10] when restricting to negative-definite Seifert fibred spaces.

2. Full paths and the height function

2.1. The full path algorithm

Throughout the paper we assume that the reader is familiar with the basics of Heegaard Floer homology [OSz-involution]. Since we are mainly interested in Seifert fibred spaces, we only work with the Heegaard Floer homology of graph manifolds. The Heegaard Floer groups of these 3-manifolds have been studied in depth [OSz-fullpath, Nemethi, DM, Lattice2], and were fruitfully applied in 3-dimensional contact topology [LS-existance, Karakurt, Irena, BP].

We now review the full path algorithm of Ozsváth and Szabó [OSz-fullpath], on which our results mainly rely. We use the same terminology, and conventions as in their paper.

Let Γ\Gamma be a negative-definite tree which can be turn into an LL-space by lowering the framing of one vertex; such a graph is called almost-rational, see [Nemethi0, Nemethi]. We write P=X​(Γ)P=X(\Gamma) for the corresponding plumbing 4-manifold, and YY for its boundary rational homology sphere. Since PP is simply connected, one can identify a Spinc\operatorname{Spin}^{c}-structure 𝔲∈Spinc⁡(P)\mathfrak{u}\in\operatorname{Spin}^{c}(P) with its first Chern class c1​(𝔲)∈H2​(P;ℤ)c_{1}(\mathfrak{u})\in H^{2}(P;\mathbb{Z}). Furthermore, since the spheres S1,…,S|Γ|S_{1},\dots,S_{|\Gamma|} associated to the vertices of the plumbing Γ\Gamma form a basis of H2​(P;ℤ)H_{2}(P;\mathbb{Z}), we have a natural identification H2​(P;ℤ)≃ℤ|Γ|H^{2}(P;\mathbb{Z})\simeq\mathbb{Z}^{|\Gamma|}. This allows us to represent the Chern class c1​(𝔲)c_{1}(\mathfrak{u}) of a Spinc\operatorname{Spin}^{c}-structure 𝔲\mathfrak{u} on the plumbing with a vector V=(v1,…,v|Γ|)V=(v_{1},\dots,v_{|\Gamma|}), where vi=⟨c1​(𝔲),[Si]⟩v_{i}=\langle c_{1}(\mathfrak{u}),[S_{i}]\rangle. Any vector V∈ℤ|Γ|V\in\mathbb{Z}^{|\Gamma|} of this kind is called characteristic vector, and is characterised by the property that vi≡m​(i)​ mod ​2v_{i}\equiv m(i)\text{ mod }2, where m​(i)m(i) denotes the framing of the ii-th vertex of Γ\Gamma.

Since PP is a negative-definite 4-manifold with b1​(P)=0b_{1}(P)=0, it follows from the properties of the cobordism maps [OSz-negative, Proposition 9.4] that FP,𝔲−:H​F−​(S3)→H​F−​(Y)F^{-}_{P,\mathfrak{u}}:HF^{-}(S^{3})\rightarrow HF^{-}(Y) gives rise to a non-torsion element FP,𝔲−​(1)∈H​F−​(Y)F^{-}_{P,\mathfrak{u}}(1)\in HF^{-}(Y). In [OSz-fullpath] Ozsváth and Szabó show that each element FP,𝔲−​(1)F^{-}_{P,\mathfrak{u}}(1) is identified with the equivalence class [V][V], of the characteristic vector VV associated to 𝔲\mathfrak{u}, determined by the full path algorithm, which we now describe.

In what follows we denote by QQ the intersection form of PP. In the basis {[S1],…,[S|Γ|]}\{[S_{1}],...,[S_{|\Gamma|}]\} one has that Qi​j=[Si]⋅[Sj]=|Si⋔Sj|Q_{ij}=[S_{i}]\cdot[S_{j}]=|S_{i}\pitchfork S_{j}|. Rephrasing [OSz-fullpath] we say that a characteristic vector VV:

    initiates its full path if m​(i)+2⩽vi⩽−m​(i)m(i)+2\leqslant v_{i}\leqslant-m(i) for each i=1,…,|Γ|i=1,...,|\Gamma|;

  • •

    terminates its full path if m​(i)⩽vi⩽−m​(i)−2m(i)\leqslant v_{i}\leqslant-m(i)-2 for each i=1,…,|Γ|i=1,...,|\Gamma|;

  • •

    originates a step to V′=V+2​PD​[Sj]V^{\prime}=V+2\text{PD}[S_{j}] if vj=−m​(j)v_{j}=-m(j) for some jj;

Two vectors are in the same full path if there is a finite sequence of steps from one to the other. When a full path contains both an initial and a terminal vector, and it does not drop out, we say that it ends correctly.

Theorem 2.1 (Ozsváth-Szabó, Némethi).

Let YY be a three-manifold presented by an almost-rational graph. Then the correction term d:=d​(Y,𝔰)d:=d(Y,\mathfrak{s}) is the maximal Maslov grading of a non-zero homogeneous element in H​F−​(Y,𝔰)HF^{-}(Y,\mathfrak{s}). Furthermore, the equivalence classes of vectors (the full paths) generate H​F−​(Y)HF^{-}(Y) as an 𝔽​[U]\mathbb{F}[U]-module; among these, the full paths that end correctly form a basis of H​F−​(Y)/U⋅H​F−​(Y)HF^{-}(Y)/U\cdot HF^{-}(Y) as an 𝔽\mathbb{F}-vector space.

Proof.

Ozsváth and Szabó [OSz-fullpath, Theorem 1.2 and Proposition 3.2] and Némethi [Nemethi0, Theorem 8.3] show that the kernel of the UU-multiplication Ker⁡U⊂H​F+​(−Y)\operatorname{Ker}U\subset HF^{+}(-Y) is dual to the vector space spanned by the full paths that end correctly. The fact that Ker⁡U≃H​F−​(Y)/U⋅H​F−​(Y)\operatorname{Ker}U\simeq HF^{-}(Y)/U\cdot HF^{-}(Y) follows from the canonical duality H​F+​(−Y)≃H​F−​(Y)∙HF^{+}(-Y)\simeq HF^{-}(Y)^{\bullet} established in [OSz-involution, Proposition 2.5], see also Subsection LABEL:subsection:duality below. ∎

Proposition 2.2.

Let ψ∗\psi_{*} be the map induced in homology by the projection of C​F−​(Y,𝔰)CF^{-}(Y,\mathfrak{s}) over the quotient complex C​F^​(Y,𝔰):=C​F−​(Y,𝔰)/U=0\widehat{CF}(Y,\mathfrak{s}):=CF^{-}(Y,\mathfrak{s})/\>U=0. As we mentioned above the homology classes corresponding to characteristic vectors are non-torsion with respect to the 𝔽​[U]\mathbb{F}[U]-module structure; hence, by Theorem 2.1 we see that H​F−​(Y,𝔰)HF^{-}(Y,\mathfrak{s}) is supported in Maslov gradings with the same parity of dd. From the first homomorphism theorem, we just need to show that Im⁡ψ∗=H​F^∗ev​(Y,𝔰)\operatorname{Im}\psi_{*}=\widehat{HF}^{\text{ev}}_{*}(Y,\mathfrak{s}); this and the second claim in the statement both follow from this fact: every non-zero class α∈H​F^​(Y,𝔰)\alpha\in\widehat{HF}(Y,\mathfrak{s}) such that either M​(α)⩾dM(\alpha)\geqslant d or α∈H​F^ev​(Y,𝔰)\alpha\in\widehat{HF}^{\text{ev}}(Y,\mathfrak{s}) is in Im⁡ψ∗\operatorname{Im}\psi_{*}.

Indeed, one can search for a collection of characteristic vectors {V1,…,Vt}\{V_{1},...,V_{t}\} restricting to the Spinc\operatorname{Spin}^{c}-structure 𝔰\mathfrak{s}, and find all possible full paths using Ozsváth and Szabó algorithm. 2.2. The conjugation involution

In what follows we exploit a certain symmetry of the Heegaard Floer chain complex. We recall that there is a map 𝒥:H​F∘​(Y)→H​F∘​(Y)\mathcal{J}:HF^{\circ}(Y)\to HF^{\circ}(Y) obtained by canonically identifying the intersection points of a given Heegaard diagram (Σ,α,β)(\Sigma,\alpha,\beta) of YY to the ones of the conjugate diagram (−Σ,β,α)(-\Sigma,\beta,\alpha), where the orientation of the Heegaard surface is flipped, and the role of the α\alpha- and the β\beta-curves swapped [OSz-involution, Theorem 2.4]. Note that 𝒥\mathcal{J} preserves the Maslov grading, squares to the identity, and maps H​F∘​(Y,𝔰)HF^{\circ}(Y,\mathfrak{s}) to H​F∘​(Y,𝔰¯)HF^{\circ}(Y,\overline{\mathfrak{s}}).

It was noted by Hendricks and Manolescu [HM] that, composing the latter identification with the chain map induced by a sequence of Heegaard moves connecting the two Heegaard diagrams, one obtains an involution denoted by ι\iota on the chain complex itself. They showed that ι\iota induces 𝒥\mathcal{J} in homology; in other words, one has ι∗=𝒥\iota_{*}=\mathcal{J}. In this paper we work with the involution 𝒥\mathcal{J}, as we are only interested in the behaviour at the homology level.

One can compute H​F−​(Y,𝔰)HF^{-}(Y,\mathfrak{s}) using the Ozsváth and Szabó full path algorithm; indeed, in this set-up the conjugation involution 𝒥\mathcal{J} acts on full paths by negating characteristic vectors, that is 𝒥​[V]=[−V]\mathcal{J}[V]=[-V] for any VV, as shown by Dai and Manolescu [DM, Theorem 3.1], for manifolds presented by an almost-rational graph. The involution 𝒥\mathcal{J} was instrumental in Ghiggini’s identification of strongly fillable contact manifolds without Stein fillings [G-notStein].
