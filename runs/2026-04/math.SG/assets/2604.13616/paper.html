Topics in Magnetic Geometry:
interpolation, intersections and integrability

Lina Deschamps Faculty of Mathematics and Computer Science, University of Heidelberg, Im Neuenheimer Field 205, 69120 Heidelberg, Germany ldeschamps@mathi.uni-heidelberg.de , Levin Maier Faculty of Mathematics and Computer Science, University of Heidelberg, Im Neuenheimer Field 205, 69120 Heidelberg, Germany lmaier@mathi.uni-heidelberg.de and Tom Stalljohann Faculty of Mathematics and Computer Science, University of Heidelberg, Im Neuenheimer Field 205, 69120 Heidelberg, Germany tstalljohann@mathi.uni-heidelberg.de
Abstract.

This paper develops new links between contact geometry, magnetic dynamics, and symmetry in exact magnetic systems.

First, we establish an interpolation property for Killing magnetic systems on contact manifolds under an additional condition. Specifically, we show that the corresponding magnetic geodesic flow interpolates smoothly between the sub-Riemannian geodesic flow on the contact distribution and the flow of the vector field associated with a primitive of the magnetic field.

Second, we show that Hamiltonian group actions associated with the magnetomorphism group produce Poisson-commuting integrals of motion for the magnetic flow.

Finally, we obtain new structural results on totally magnetic submanifolds, showing that fixed-point sets of magnetomorphisms and intersections of totally magnetic submanifolds are again totally magnetic. The latter two results may be viewed as extensions of classical phenomena from Riemannian geometry to magnetic geometry.

1. Introduction

After the pioneering work of Arnold [5] in the 1960s, which placed the motion of a charged particle in a magnetic field into the framework of modern dynamical systems, magnetic flows have attracted considerable attention; see, for instance, [2, 3, 7, 6, 11, 15, 19, 23, 28, 30, 31, 32].

Magnetic geodesic systems.

Let (M,g)(M,g) be a closed, connected Riemannian manifold and σ∈Ω2​(M)\sigma\in\Omega^{2}(M) a closed two-form. The form σ\sigma is called a magnetic field, and the triple (M,g,σ)(M,g,\sigma) is called a magnetic system. It determines a skew-symmetric bundle endomorphism Y:T​M→T​MY\colon TM\to TM, the Lorentz force, by

gq​(Yq​u,v)=σq​(u,v),∀q∈M,∀u,v∈Tq​M.g_{q}\left(Y_{q}u,v\right)=\sigma_{q}(u,v),\qquad\forall\,q\in M,\ \forall\,u,v\in T_{q}M. (1.1)

A smooth curve γ:ℝ→M\gamma\colon{\mathbb{R}}\to M satisfying

∇γ˙γ˙=Yγ​γ˙\nabla_{\dot{\gamma}}\dot{\gamma}=Y_{\gamma}\dot{\gamma} (1.2)

is called a magnetic geodesic of (M,g,σ)(M,g,\sigma). Here ∇\nabla denotes the Levi-Civita connection of gg. When σ=0\sigma=0, this reduces to the usual geodesic equation. A central question is therefore to understand similarities and differences between standard and magnetic geodesics.

Since YY is skew-symmetric, magnetic geodesics preserve the kinetic energy

E​(γ,γ˙):=12​gγ​(γ˙,γ˙),E(\gamma,\dot{\gamma}):=\tfrac{1}{2}g_{\gamma}(\dot{\gamma},\dot{\gamma}),

and hence have constant speed. This reflects the Hamiltonian nature of the system. Indeed, the magnetic geodesic flow is defined by

Φg,σt:T​M→T​M,(q,v)↦(γq,v​(t),γ˙q,v​(t)),∀t∈ℝ,\varPhi_{g,\sigma}^{t}\colon TM\to TM,\quad(q,v)\mapsto\left(\gamma_{q,v}(t),\dot{\gamma}_{q,v}(t)\right),\quad\forall t\in{\mathbb{R}},

where γq,v\gamma_{q,v} is the magnetic geodesic with initial condition (q,v)(q,v). As shown in [18], this flow is Hamiltonian with Hamiltonian EE and twisted symplectic form

ωσ=d​λ−πT​M∗​σ.\omega_{\sigma}={\mathrm{d}}\lambda-\pi^{*}_{TM}\sigma. (1.3)

A key difference from the geodesic flow is that magnetic geodesics with different speeds cannot be obtained from one another by reparametrization. This motivates the study of the flow on varying energy levels.

Magnetic geometry.

Magnetic systems admit a geometric interpretation in which classical notions from Riemannian geometry—such as curvature, isometry groups, and totally geodesic submanifolds—have natural magnetic counterparts and interact with the dynamics.

While the notion of magnetic curvature has recently been introduced in [7], our focus is on the relation between symmetry and invariant submanifolds. In [4], Albers, Benedetti, and the second author introduced totally magnetic submanifolds and the magnetomorphism group. The former are submanifolds invariant under the magnetic flow; see 2.1. The latter consist of diffeomorphisms preserving both gg and σ\sigma; see 2.3. These parallels are summarized in Table 1, which also highlights the magnetic counterparts established in the present work.

The study of totally magnetic submanifolds has attracted further attention in [33, 26] and has found applications to nonlinear PDEs, notably the magnetic two-component Hunter–Saxton system [21]. This provides additional motivation for the present work.

Main contributions.

We establish the following results:

  • •

    Interpolation property. For Killing magnetic systems on contact manifolds, we prove that, under a natural compatibility condition, the magnetic geodesic flow interpolates between the sub-Riemannian geodesic flow on (M,ξ=ker⁡α)(M,\xi=\ker\alpha) and the flow of the vector field dual to α\alpha; see 3.2.

  • •

    Integrals of motion from symmetry. For exact magnetic systems, we show that Hamiltonian group actions associated with the magnetomorphism group yield Poisson-commuting, functionally independent integrals of motion, with cardinality at least the dimension of an enclosed torus; see 5.2 and 5.11.

  • •

    Structure of totally magnetic submanifolds. We prove that fixed-point sets of magnetomorphisms are totally magnetic submanifolds and that intersections of totally magnetic submanifolds remain totally magnetic; see 6.1 and 6.2.

2. Preliminaries

2.1. Variational description of magnetic geodesic flows

In the case of an exact magnetic system (M,g,d​α)(M,g,{\mathrm{d}}\alpha), the magnetic geodesic flow admits a Lagrangian description, and hence a variational formulation. The associated magnetic Lagrangian is

L:T​M→ℝ,L​(q,v):=12​|v|2−αq​(v).L\colon TM\to\mathbb{R},\qquad L(q,v):=\tfrac{1}{2}|v|^{2}-\alpha_{q}(v).

The magnetic geodesic flow Φg,d​αt\Phi^{t}_{g,{\mathrm{d}}\alpha} coincides with the Euler–Lagrange flow ΦL\Phi_{L} associated with the magnetic Lagrangian LL; see [18]. More precisely, a curve γ:[0,T]→M\gamma\colon[0,T]\to M is a magnetic geodesic if and only if it is a critical point of the action functional

SL​(γ):=∫0TL​(γ​(t),γ˙​(t))​dtS_{L}(\gamma):=\int_{0}^{T}L(\gamma(t),\dot{\gamma}(t))\,{\mathrm{d}}t

among all smooth curves δ:[0,T]→M\delta\colon[0,T]\to M satisfying

δ​(0)=γ​(0),δ​(T)=γ​(T).\delta(0)=\gamma(0),\qquad\delta(T)=\gamma(T).

This variational principle prescribes the time interval [0,T][0,T], while the energy of γ\gamma is left free. On the other hand, for a fixed energy level κ∈ℝ\kappa\in\mathbb{R}, a curve γ\gamma is a magnetic geodesic of energy κ\kappa if and only if it is a critical point of the free-time action functional SL+κS_{L+\kappa}, where both the curve and the travel time are allowed to vary. More precisely, one considers variations among smooth curves δ:[0,T′]→M\delta\colon[0,T^{\prime}]\to M with arbitrary T′>0T^{\prime}>0 such that

δ​(0)=γ​(0),δ​(T′)=γ​(T).\delta(0)=\gamma(0),\qquad\delta(T^{\prime})=\gamma(T).

We conclude this subsection by recalling that a magnetic geodesic γ\gamma of (M,g,d​α)(M,g,{\mathrm{d}}\alpha) is said to have negative action if

SL+κ​(γ)<0,S_{L+\kappa}(\gamma)<0,

where LL is the magnetic Lagrangian of (M,g,d​α)(M,g,{\mathrm{d}}\alpha) and κ\kappa is the energy of γ\gamma.

2.2. Mañé’s critical values

This variational formulation underlies the definition of Mañé’s critical values, introduced in the seminal works [11, 19]. These quantities may be interpreted as energy levels at which significant dynamical and geometric transitions occur in the Euler–Lagrange flow associated with the magnetic Lagrangian LL. For simplicity, we restrict here to the definition of the Mañé critical value, defined by

c​(L):=inf{κ∈ℝ|SL+κ​(γ)≥0​ for all ​T>0​ and all ​γ∈C∞​(ℝ/T​ℤ,M)}.c(L):=\inf\left\{\kappa\in\mathbb{R}\,\middle|\,S_{L+\kappa}(\gamma)\geq 0\text{ for all }T>0\text{ and all }\gamma\in C^{\infty}(\mathbb{R}/T\mathbb{Z},M)\right\}. (2.1)

For an overview of other Mañé critical values, we refer to [1] and the references therein. Finally, we note that the Mañé critical value can also be defined for non-exact magnetic fields, following [10, 23], although this generalization lies beyond the scope of the present paper.

2.3. Background in magnetic geometry.

In Riemannian geometry, the study of totally geodesic submanifolds is a classical topic. In [4], Albers, Benedetti, and the second author initiated the analogous study in magnetic geometry by introducing the notion of so-called totally magnetic submanifolds. The reader may think of them as the analogue of totally geodesic submanifolds for magnetic geodesic flows, in the sense that they are precisely the submanifolds that are invariant under the magnetic geodesic flow, just as totally geodesic submanifolds are precisely the submanifolds invariant under the geodesic flow.

We begin by recalling the notion of a totally magnetic submanifold.

Definition 2.1 (Totally magnetic submanifold, [4, Def. 6.5]).

Let (M,g,σ)(M,g,\sigma) be a magnetic system. An (embedded) submanifold N⊆MN\subseteq M is called totally magnetic if for every magnetic geodesic γ:I→M\gamma:I\to M with γ​(0)∈N\gamma(0)\in N and γ˙​(0)∈Tγ​(0)​N\dot{\gamma}(0)\in T_{\gamma(0)}N, there exists ε>0\varepsilon>0 such that γ​((−ε,ε))⊆N\gamma((-\varepsilon,\varepsilon))\subseteq N.

Before proceeding, we recall the following useful criterion for determining whether a submanifold is totally magnetic.

Theorem 2.2 ([4], Thm. 1.4).

Let (M,g,σ)(M,g,\sigma) be a magnetic system with Lorentz force Y:T​M→T​MY\colon TM\to TM, and let N⊆MN\subseteq M be a submanifold. Denote by i:N↪Mi\colon N\hookrightarrow M the inclusion. The following are equivalent:

  1. (i)

    NN is a totally magnetic submanifold of (M,g,σ)(M,g,\sigma).

  2. (ii)

    Every magnetic geodesic γ:I→N\gamma\colon I\to N of (N,i∗​g,i∗​σ)(N,i^{*}g,i^{*}\sigma) is also a magnetic geodesic of (M,g,σ)(M,g,\sigma).

  3. (iii)

    NN is a totally geodesic submanifold of (M,g)(M,g) and Yp​(Tp​N)⊆Tp​NY_{p}(T_{p}N)\subseteq T_{p}N for all p∈Np\in N.

Typically, totally geodesic submanifolds are classified up to isometry. For magnetic systems, the analogous notion is a structure-preserving map of magnetic systems, namely a magnetomorphism, which is defined as follows.

Definition 2.3 (Magnetomorphism group, [4, Def. 6.2]).

Given two magnetic systems (M1,g1,σ1)(M_{1},g_{1},\sigma_{1}) and (M2,g2,σ2)(M_{2},g_{2},\sigma_{2}), the two systems are called magnetomorphic if there exists a diffeomorphism Φ:M1→M2\varPhi:M_{1}\to M_{2} such that

Φ∗​g2=g1andΦ∗​σ2=σ1.\varPhi^{*}g_{2}=g_{1}\quad\text{and}\quad\varPhi^{*}\sigma_{2}=\sigma_{1}\,.

Such a map is called a magnetomorphism.

For a magnetic system (M,g,σ)(M,g,\sigma), the group of magnetomorphisms is

Mag​(M,g,σ):={ψ∈Diff​(M)|ψ∗​g=g​ and ​ψ∗​σ=σ}.\displaystyle\mathrm{Mag}(M,g,\sigma):=\left\{\,\psi\in\mathrm{Diff}(M)\,|\,\psi^{*}g=g\text{ and }\psi^{*}\sigma=\sigma\,\right\}\,.

2.4. Contact geometry

Natural choices for magnetic forms are symplectic forms or contact forms, where the latter arise in contact geometry, the odd-dimensional analogue of symplectic geometry. We begin by recalling the basic notions.

A contact manifold is a pair (M,α)(M,\alpha), where MM is a closed (2​n+1)(2n+1)-dimensional manifold and α∈Ω1​(M)\alpha\in\Omega^{1}(M) is a 11-form such that

α∧(d​α)n≠0\alpha\wedge({\mathrm{d}}\alpha)^{n}\neq 0

everywhere on MM. There exists a unique vector field RR, called the Reeb vector field, such that

α​(R)=1,ιR​d​α=0.\alpha(R)=1,\qquad\iota_{R}{\mathrm{d}}\alpha=0.

The contact form α\alpha gives rise to the contact distribution ξ:=ker⁡α\xi:=\ker\alpha, which is a completely non-integrable hyperplane distribution. Moreover, one has the splitting

T​M=ξ⊕⟨R⟩ℝTM=\xi\oplus\langle R\rangle_{{\mathbb{R}}}

and the kernel of d​α{\mathrm{d}}\alpha is precisely the line bundle generated by RR, that is,

ker⁡d​α=⟨R⟩ℝ.\ker{\mathrm{d}}\alpha=\langle R\rangle_{{\mathbb{R}}}.

For more details on contact geometry we refer the reader to [17]. It is natural to consider contact structures that are compatible with a Riemannian metric. This leads, in particular, to the notion of a KK-contact structure, which links contact geometry with Riemannian geometry; see [9].

A KK-contact structure on MM is a pair (g,α)(g,\alpha), where α\alpha is a contact form and gg is a Riemannian metric satisfying the following properties:

  1. (K1)

    the contact distribution ker⁡α\ker\alpha is gg-orthogonal to the Reeb vector field RR;

  2. (K2)

    the Reeb vector field RR is a Killing vector field for gg and has constant norm r>0r>0.

3. Geometry of Killing magnetic systems

This section focuses on Killing magnetic systems, and illustrates the strong geometric constraints on magnetic trajectories imposed by the Killing condition. We show an interpolation property of the magnetic geodesic flow on Killing magnetic systems, which is of independent interest, particularly considering applications to KK-contact manifolds.

We recall the definition of a Killing magnetic system introduced in [4].

Definition 3.1 ([4, Def. 2.1.]).

An exact magnetic system (M,g,d​α)(M,g,{\mathrm{d}}\alpha) on a closed manifold MM is called Killing if the gg-dual vector field XX of α\alpha is a Killing vector field, that is, the flow of XX acts by isometries on (M,g)(M,g). In this case, we say that α\alpha is Killing with respect to gg, and that the associated magnetic Lagrangian LL is Killing.

3.1. The Interpolation for Killing Magnetic Systems

In this subsection, we study an interpolation property, generalizing [4, Cor. 1.8]. We characterize the Killing magnetic systems whose flow interpolates between a sub-Riemannian geodesic flow and a Reeb flow. For background on sub-Riemannian geometry see [24].

This property can be detected by the angle between XX, the vector field dual to the magnetic 11-form α\alpha, and the Reeb direction along magnetic geodesics. We show that it holds precisely when the norm of XX stays constant along magnetic geodesics.

Theorem 3.2 (Interpolation property).

Let (M2​n+1,α)(M^{2n+1},\alpha) be a closed contact manifold equipped with a Riemannian metric gg, and suppose that the magnetic system (M,g,d​α)(M,g,\mathrm{d}\alpha) is Killing. If the gg-metric dual XX of α\alpha satisfies

d​α​(X,⋅)=0,{\mathrm{d}}\alpha(X,\cdot)=0\,, (3.1)

then the gγ​(Xγ,γ˙)g_{\gamma}(X_{\gamma},\dot{\gamma}) is conserved along magnetic geodesics γ\gamma of (M,g,d​α)(M,g,{\mathrm{d}}\alpha).
Moreover, the magnetic geodesic flow of (M,g,d​α)(M,g,\mathrm{d}\alpha) interpolates smoothly between the sub-Riemannian geodesic flow on the distribution ξ:=ker⁡α\xi:=\ker\alpha and the flow of XX.

More specifically:

  1. (1)

    If γ\gamma is a magnetic geodesic with initial velocity γ˙​(0)∈ξγ​(0)\dot{\gamma}(0)\in\xi_{\gamma(0)}, then γ\gamma is a sub-Riemannian geodesic tangent to the distribution ξ\xi.

  2. (2)

    If γ\gamma is a magnetic geodesic with initial velocity γ˙​(0)=r⋅Xγ​(0)\dot{\gamma}(0)=r\cdot X_{\gamma(0)} for some r∈ℝr\in\mathbb{R}, then γ\gamma is a flow line of the vector field r⋅Xr\cdot X.

Remark 3.3.

As a special case of 3.2, we recover the recent result [4, Cor. 1.8] of the second author, which provides the first example of a higher-dimensional magnetic system having the interpolation property.

Remark 3.4.

Such an interpolation phenomenon is specific to magnetic dynamics: for the ordinary geodesic flow of a Riemannian metric, there is in general no analogous mechanism interpolating between a sub-Riemannian geodesic flow on a hyperplane distribution and the flow of a distinguished vector field.

Proof.

By differentiating gγ​(Xγ,γ˙)g_{\gamma}(X_{\gamma},\dot{\gamma}) with respect to tt and using that γ\gamma is a magnetic geodesic of (M,g,d​α)(M,g,{\mathrm{d}}\alpha), hence a solution of (1.2), we obtain

Since the magnetic system (M,g,d​α)(M,g,{\mathrm{d}}\alpha) is Killing, we have, by [4, S. 2.2],

Y=2​∇X.Y=2\nabla X\,. (3.3)

Let γ\gamma be a magnetic geodesic with initial velocity γ˙​(0)∈ξγ​(0)=ker⁡αγ​(0)\dot{\gamma}(0)\in\xi_{\gamma(0)}=\ker\alpha_{\gamma(0)}, i.e., γ\gamma is as in (1). 3.2. Applications of the interpolation property

This subsection presents KK-contact manifolds as examples of magnetic systems to which 3.2 applies.

Corollary 3.5.

Let (g,α)(g,\alpha) be a KK-contact structure on a smooth closed manifold MM. Then the magnetic geodesic flow of (M,g,d​α)(M,g,{\mathrm{d}}\alpha) interpolates between the sub-Riemannian geodesic flow on (M,ξ:=ker⁡α)(M,\xi:=\ker\alpha) and the Reeb flow of (M,α)(M,\alpha).

Proof.

By [4, §2.3], specifically the discussion immediately preceding [4, Theorem 2.6], if (M,g,α)(M,g,\alpha) is a KK-contact manifold, then the assumptions of 3.2 are satisfied. The claimed interpolation statement therefore follows directly from 3.2. ∎

4. Illustration on Ellipsoids

The aim of this section is to illustrate Section 3 by providing an example of a magnetic system on a contact manifold that is Killing, but whose Reeb flow does not act by isometries. This section is divided into three parts. In the first part, Section 4.1, we approach these systems from a spherical perspective. This allows us to identify the magnetic system on the ellipsoid with a suitable magnetic system on the unit sphere in 4.1. We then show in 4.2 that this system is Killing, which allows us to compute its Mañé critical value. Next, in Section 4.2, we use this point of view to construct integrals of motion for the corresponding electromagnetic Lagrangians; see 4.4. Finally, in Section 4.3, we conclude with an application of independent interest, namely to the contact type problem for low-energy surfaces.

4.3. The contact type property of the magnetic system E​(A)E(A)

We close this subsection with an observation of independent interest concerning the magnetic system (E​(A),g,d​α)(E(A),g,{\mathrm{d}}\alpha), which has been a guiding example in the authors’ work [13, 14].

5. Further aspects of magnetic geometry: integrability and magnetic symmetries

In this section we investigate the relation between magnetic symmetries and integrability. More precisely, in Section 5.1 we show that Hamiltonian group actions associated with the magnetomorphism group produce Poisson-commuting integrals of motion for the magnetic geodesic flow. In Section 5.2 we introduce the exact magnetomorphism group to which we apply the previous mechanism. This will be illustrated in Section 5.3 on complex ellipsoids, homogeneous spaces and spheres of revolution.

5.1. Integrals of motion from magnetic symmetries

Let (M,g,d​α)(M,g,{\mathrm{d}}\alpha) be an exact magnetic system on a closed manifold. We retain the notation from Section 2; in particular, T​MTM is equipped with the twisted symplectic form ωd​α\omega_{{\mathrm{d}}\alpha}.

If G⊆Isom​(M,g)G\subseteq\mathrm{Isom}(M,g) is a closed Lie subgroup, we consider its natural lifted action on T​MTM and identify 𝔤=Lie​(G)\mathfrak{g}=\mathrm{Lie}(G) with the corresponding infinitesimal generators on MM.

Subsequently, it will be useful to be aware of the following expression for the infinitesimal generator of the lifted action.

Lemma 5.1.

For X∈𝔤X\in\mathfrak{g}, the infinitesimal generator X#∈𝔛​(T​M)X^{\#}\in\mathfrak{X}(TM) of the lifted action is given by

X#​(q,v)=(X​(q),∇vX)X^{\#}(q,v)=(X(q),\nabla_{v}X)

with respect to the Levi–Civita splitting T(q,v)​T​M≅Tq​M⊕Tq​MT_{(q,v)}TM\cong T_{q}M\oplus T_{q}M.

Proof.

This is the standard expression for the tangent lift with respect to the connection-induced splitting of T​T​MTTM. ∎

We now state the general mechanism producing integrals of motion from magnetic symmetries.

Theorem 5.2 (Integrals of motion from magnetic symmetries).

Let (M,g,d​α)(M,g,{\mathrm{d}}\alpha) be an exact magnetic system on a closed manifold, and let

G⊆Mag​(M,g,d​α)G\subseteq\mathrm{Mag}(M,g,{\mathrm{d}}\alpha)

be a closed Lie subgroup whose induced action on (T​M,ωd​α)(TM,\omega_{{\mathrm{d}}\alpha}) is Hamiltonian, with moment map μ:T​M→𝔤∗\mu:TM\to\mathfrak{g}^{*}.

If T⊆GT\subseteq G is a torus with Lie algebra 𝔱\mathfrak{t}, then for every X∈𝔱X\in\mathfrak{t} the function

FX:T​M⟶ℝ,(q,v)↦⟨μ​(q,v),X⟩F_{X}:TM\longrightarrow{\mathbb{R}},\quad(q,v)\mapsto\langle\mu(q,v),X\rangle

is an integral of motion of the magnetic geodesic flow. Moreover, for all X,Y∈𝔱X,Y\in\mathfrak{t} one has

{FX,FY}=0.\{F_{X},F_{Y}\}=0.

In particular, any basis of 𝔱\mathfrak{t} yields a family of Poisson-commuting first integrals, which are independent on an open dense subset of T​MTM whenever the corresponding infinitesimal generators are. If dimT<dimM\dim T<\dim M, the kinetic energy can be included as an additional independent integral.

Remark 5.3.

5.2 applies whenever the induced action on (T​M,ωd​α)(TM,\omega_{{\mathrm{d}}\alpha}) is Hamiltonian. In the following subsection we show that this is the case for the exact magnetomorphism group.

Remark 5.4.

The Hamiltonianity assumption is natural. Indeed, in the classical case of the isometry group acting on the tangent bundle of a Riemannian manifold equipped with its canonical symplectic structure, the induced action is always Hamiltonian, and the associated moment map recovers the usual Noether integrals of motion. Thus, 5.2 should be viewed as the magnetic analogue of this standard picture.

Proof.

Since GG acts by isometries, the induced action on T​MTM preserves the kinetic energy

E​(q,v)=12​gq​(v,v),E(q,v)=\tfrac{1}{2}g_{q}(v,v),

whose Hamiltonian flow is the magnetic geodesic flow. Hence, by Noether’s theorem, each FXF_{X} Poisson-commutes with EE.

Equivariance of the moment map yields

{FX,FY}=⟨μ,[X,Y]⟩∀X,Y∈𝔱,\{F_{X},F_{Y}\}=\langle\mu,[X,Y]\rangle\qquad\forall X,Y\in\mathfrak{t},

which vanishes since 𝔱\mathfrak{t} is abelian. The independence statements follow from the fact that the Hamiltonian vector field of FXF_{X} is the infinitesimal generator X#X^{\#}.

If dim(T)<dim(M)\dim(T)<\dim(M)\,, then the Hamiltonian vector field XEX_{E} of the energy is linearly independent from the infinitesimal generators of a basis of 𝔱\mathfrak{t} on a dense subset of T​MTM. Indeed, the pushforward of XE​(q,v)X_{E}(q,v) via πT​M\pi_{TM} is vv, while the pushforward of the infinitesimal generators only depends on qq, see Lemma 5.1. ∎

5.2. The exact magnetomorphism group

5.2 reduces the construction of first integrals to the existence of a Hamiltonian action on (T​M,ωd​α)(TM,\omega_{{\mathrm{d}}\alpha}). We now show that, for exact magnetic systems, the exact magnetomorphism group provides such an action canonically, which we introduce for this purpose.

Definition 5.5 (Exact magnetomorphism group).

Given two exact magnetic systems (M1,g1,d​α1)(M_{1},g_{1},{\mathrm{d}}\alpha_{1}) and (M2,g2,d​α2)(M_{2},g_{2},{\mathrm{d}}\alpha_{2}), the two systems are called exact magnetomorphic if there exists a diffeomorphism Φ:M1→M2\varPhi:M_{1}\to M_{2} such that

Φ∗​g2=g1andΦ∗​α2=α1.\varPhi^{*}g_{2}=g_{1}\quad\text{and}\quad\varPhi^{*}\alpha_{2}=\alpha_{1}\,.

Such a map is called a exact magnetomorphism.

For an exact magnetic system (M,g,d​α)(M,g,{\mathrm{d}}\alpha), the exact magnetomorphism group is defined by

Magex​(M,g,α):={ψ∈Diff​(M)∣ψ∗​g=g​ and ​ψ∗​α=α}.\mathrm{Mag}_{\mathrm{ex}}(M,g,\alpha):=\{\psi\in\mathrm{Diff}(M)\mid\psi^{*}g=g\text{ and }\psi^{*}\alpha=\alpha\}.

First, we observe that the subgroup

Magex​(M,g,α)⊆Mag​(M,g,d​α)\mathrm{Mag}_{\mathrm{ex}}(M,g,\alpha)\subseteq\mathrm{Mag}(M,g,{\mathrm{d}}\alpha)

is closed. We next identify its Lie algebra.

Lemma 5.6.

The Lie algebra of the group of exact magnetomorphisms is given by

Lie​(Magex​(M,g,α))={X∈𝔛​(M)∣X​ is Killing and ​ℒX​α=0}.\mathrm{Lie}(\mathrm{Mag}_{\mathrm{ex}}(M,g,\alpha))=\{X\in\mathfrak{X}(M)\mid X\text{ is Killing and }\mathcal{L}_{X}\alpha=0\}.
Remark 5.7.

5.6 also holds in the case where (M,g)(M,g) is complete.

Proof.

Since MM is closed, (M,g)(M,g) is complete. Thus, it is well known that the Lie algebra of Isom​(M,g)\mathrm{Isom}(M,g) consists of Killing vector fields. For a Killing vector field XX on MM with its associated flow ϕXt\phi_{X}^{t}, one has the standard identity

ℒX​α=0⟺(ϕXt)∗​α=α∀t∈ℝ.\mathcal{L}_{X}\alpha=0\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad(\phi_{X}^{t})^{*}\alpha=\alpha\qquad\forall t\in\mathbb{R}.

This is equivalent to ϕXt∈Magex​(M,g,α)\phi_{X}^{t}\in\mathrm{Mag}_{\mathrm{ex}}(M,g,\alpha) for all tt, hence to XX lying in the Lie algebra. ∎

Before discussing the action of the group of exact magnetomorphisms, we state a result of independent interest.

Lemma 5.8.

Given two exact magnetic systems (M1,g1,d​α1)(M_{1},g_{1},{\mathrm{d}}\alpha_{1}) and (M2,g2,d​α2)(M_{2},g_{2},{\mathrm{d}}\alpha_{2}) that are exactly magnetomorphic, the following holds:

  1. (1)

    the Mañé critical values of (M1,g1,d​α1)(M_{1},g_{1},{\mathrm{d}}\alpha_{1}) and (M2,g2,d​α2)(M_{2},g_{2},{\mathrm{d}}\alpha_{2}) coincide,

  2. (2)

    the magnetic system (M1,g1,d​α1)(M_{1},g_{1},{\mathrm{d}}\alpha_{1}) is Killing if and only if (M2,g2,d​α2)(M_{2},g_{2},{\mathrm{d}}\alpha_{2}) is.

Remark 5.9.

First, we note that the conclusion in (1) also holds if one considers weakly exact magnetic systems; for a precise notion, we refer to [10] and the references therein.

Remark 5.10.

We stress that, if two (weakly) exact magnetic systems are magnetomorphic but not exactly magnetomorphic, then their Mañé critical values are not necessarily the same. So, in order to preserve this dynamical energy threshold, the right notion is that of the group of exact magnetomorphisms.

Proof.

This follows immediately from the definition of the group of exact magnetomorphisms, exact magnetomorphisms in 5.5 and from the definition of the Mañé critical value (2.1). ∎

6. Totally magnetic submanifolds as fixed-point sets of magnetomorphisms

In this section, we relate totally magnetic submanifolds to the magnetomorphism group. We first prove two abstract results and then apply them to strictly convex hypersurfaces in Euclidean space. This leads to a description of the magnetomorphism groups of complex ellipsoids and to a family of totally magnetic submanifolds.

6.1. Abstract theorems.

Our first result is the magnetic analogue of the classical fact in Riemannian geometry that the fixed-point set of a collection of isometries is a totally geodesic submanifold (see [25, Prop. 5.6.5]).

Proposition 6.1.

Let ℳ⊆Mag​(M,g,σ)\mathcal{M}\subseteq\mathrm{Mag}(M,g,\sigma) be a subset of magnetomorphisms. Then each connected component of the fixed-point set

Fix​(ℳ)={p∈M|F​(p)=p∀F∈ℳ}\mathrm{Fix}(\mathcal{M})=\left\{\,p\in M\,|\,F(p)=p\quad\forall\,F\in\mathcal{M}\,\right\}

is a totally magnetic submanifold of (M,g,σ)(M,g,\sigma).

To prove the proposition, we recall the definition of the Zariski tangent space. Let ℳ⊆Isom​(M,g)\mathcal{M}\subseteq\mathrm{Isom}(M,g) be a subset of isometries. For p∈Fix​(ℳ)p\in\mathrm{Fix}(\mathcal{M}), the Zariski tangent space at pp is defined by

Zp:={v∈Tp​M|dp​F⋅v=v∀F∈ℳ}⊆Tp​M.\mathrm{Z}_{p}:=\left\{\,v\in T_{p}M\,|\,{\mathrm{d}}_{p}F\cdot v=v\quad\forall\,F\in\mathcal{M}\,\right\}\subseteq T_{p}M\,. (6.1)

By [25, Prop. 5.6.5], for ε>0\varepsilon>0 sufficiently small, the map

exppM:Bε​(0)∩Zp⟶Bε​(p)∩Fix​(ℳ)\exp_{p}^{M}:B_{\varepsilon}(0)\cap\mathrm{Z}_{p}\longrightarrow B_{\varepsilon}(p)\cap\mathrm{Fix}(\mathcal{M})

is a well-defined slice chart. In particular, the connected components of Fix​(ℳ)\mathrm{Fix}(\mathcal{M}) are totally geodesic submanifolds with tangent space Zp\mathrm{Z}_{p} at pp.

Proof of 6.1.

Since ℳ⊆Mag​(M,g,σ)\mathcal{M}\subseteq\mathrm{Mag}(M,g,\sigma) consists of isometries, the above discussion implies that the connected components of Fix​(ℳ)\mathrm{Fix}(\mathcal{M}) are totally geodesic submanifolds of (M,g)(M,g). By 2.2, it remains to show that for every p∈Fix​(ℳ)p\in\mathrm{Fix}(\mathcal{M}) the Lorentz force

Yp:Tp​M⟶Tp​MY_{p}:T_{p}M\longrightarrow T_{p}M

preserves Zp\mathrm{Z}_{p}.

Let v∈Zpv\in\mathrm{Z}_{p} and F∈ℳF\in\mathcal{M} be arbitrary. Since FF is a magnetomorphism, its differential commutes with the Lorentz force, and hence

dp​F⋅Yp​(v)=Yp​(dp​F⋅v)=Yp​(v),{\mathrm{d}}_{p}F\cdot Y_{p}(v)=Y_{p}({\mathrm{d}}_{p}F\cdot v)=Y_{p}(v),

where the second equality follows from v∈Zpv\in\mathrm{Z}_{p} and (6.1).

Since FF was arbitrary, we conclude that Yp​(v)∈ZpY_{p}(v)\in\mathrm{Z}_{p}\,. ∎

The next result shows that the class of totally magnetic submanifolds is stable under intersections.

Proposition 6.2.

Let N1,N2⊆MN_{1},N_{2}\subseteq M be totally magnetic submanifolds of (M,g,σ)(M,g,\sigma). Then each connected component of

N=N1∩N2N=N_{1}\cap N_{2}

is a totally magnetic submanifold of (M,g,σ)(M,g,\sigma) with tangent space Tp​N1∩Tp​N2T_{p}N_{1}\cap T_{p}N_{2} at p∈Np\in N.

Proof of 6.2.

Let p∈N=N1∩N2p\in N=N_{1}\cap N_{2}. Since N1N_{1} and N2N_{2} are totally geodesic submanifolds, the exponential map

exppM:Bε​(0)⟶Bε​(p)\exp^{M}_{p}:B_{\varepsilon}(0)\longrightarrow B_{\varepsilon}(p)

restricts to a bijection from Bε​(0)∩Tp​N1∩Tp​N2B_{\varepsilon}(0)\cap T_{p}N_{1}\cap T_{p}N_{2} onto Bε​(p)∩NB_{\varepsilon}(p)\cap N. Hence the connected components of NN are submanifolds with tangent space Tp​N1∩Tp​N2T_{p}N_{1}\cap T_{p}N_{2} at pp.

Moreover, every magnetic geodesic of (M,g,σ)(M,g,\sigma) starting at pp with initial velocity in Tp​N1∩Tp​N2T_{p}N_{1}\cap T_{p}N_{2} remains in both N1N_{1} and N2N_{2} for small time, and thus in the connected component of NN containing pp. ∎

6.2. Illustration

7. Outlook

The results of this paper suggest several directions for further research. A particularly promising one arises from the ambient-space formulation of the magnetic geodesic equation for hypersurfaces.

More precisely, let (M,g,σ)(M,g,\sigma) be a magnetic system with Levi-Civita connection ∇\nabla and Lorentz force Y:T​M→T​MY:TM\to TM, and let Σ=f−1​(c)\Sigma=f^{-1}(c) be a regular level set of a smooth function f∈C∞​(M)f\in C^{\infty}(M). Denote by ∇f\nabla f the gradient of ff with respect to gg. Then a curve γ:(a,b)→Σ\gamma:(a,b)\to\Sigma is a magnetic geodesic of the induced magnetic system on Σ\Sigma if and only if it satisfies the ambient equation

∇γ˙γ˙=Yγ​(γ˙)+gγ​(γ˙,Yγ​(∇f​(γ))−∇γ˙∇⁡f)|∇f​(γ)|2​∇f​(γ).\nabla_{\dot{\gamma}}\dot{\gamma}=Y_{\gamma}(\dot{\gamma})+\frac{g_{\gamma}\big(\dot{\gamma},\,Y_{\gamma}(\nabla f(\gamma))-\nabla_{\dot{\gamma}}\nabla f\big)}{|\nabla f(\gamma)|^{2}}\,\nabla f(\gamma).

This formula fits into the framework of [33] and makes it possible to study magnetic geodesics on hypersurfaces directly in terms of ambient data.

In the case of complex ellipsoids, this leads to a concrete second-order ODE in the ambient space ℂn{\mathbb{C}}^{n}. Ambient formulations of the magnetic geodesic equation have already proved useful in related situations. In [4], they led to several structural insights into the dynamics, and these ideas were subsequently used by the second author in [21] in an application to a nonlinear PDE.

It would therefore be interesting to investigate whether, in the setting of Section 6.2, the ambient ODE for magnetic geodesics on ellipsoids leads to comparable new insights. This may include a better analytical understanding of the dynamics, effective numerical approaches to the magnetic flow, or possible links with nonlinear PDEs, for instance of elliptic version of a Hunter–Saxton type equation.

More broadly, the examples studied in this paper indicate that magnetic geometry on hypersurfaces may provide a useful bridge between dynamical, geometric, and analytic questions. Exploring this interaction further seems to be a natural direction for future work.
