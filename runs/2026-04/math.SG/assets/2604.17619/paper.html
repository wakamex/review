A Transverse Averaging Operator and Cohomology of Quotients by Non-closed Subgroups

Yi Lin Y. Lin
Department of Mathematical Sciences
Georgia Southern University
Statesboro, GA, 30460 USA yilin@georgiasouthern.edu
Abstract.

In this article, we introduce a transverse averaging operator for basic forms on a Riemannian foliation equipped with an isometric transverse Lie algebra action, under the assumption that the leaf closure space is compact. Unlike the classical averaging operator in equivariant geometry, which is defined by integration over a compact Lie group, our operator is built purely from infinitesimal transverse data and does not require any global group action. We show that it sends every closed basic form to an invariant basic form representing the same basic cohomology class.

As a main application, we compute the diffeological de Rham cohomology of the homogeneous space G/HG/H, where GG is a connected Lie group, not necessarily compact, and HH is a connected Lie subgroup, not necessarily closed. Let 𝔤\mathfrak{g} and 𝔥\mathfrak{h} be the Lie algebras of GG and HH, respectively. Assuming that 𝔤\mathfrak{g} is of compact type and that G/H¯G/\overline{H} is compact, we prove that

Hd​R∙​(G/H)≅H∙​(𝔤,𝔥).H^{\bullet}_{dR}(G/H)\cong H^{\bullet}(\mathfrak{g},\mathfrak{h}).

If, in addition, 𝔥\mathfrak{h} is an ideal in 𝔤\mathfrak{g}, then under the weaker assumption that G/H¯G/\overline{H} is compact, we obtain

Hd​R∙​(G/H)≅H∙​(𝔤/𝔥),H^{\bullet}_{dR}(G/H)\cong H^{\bullet}(\mathfrak{g}/\mathfrak{h}),

without requiring 𝔤\mathfrak{g} to be of compact type.

1. Introduction

Reinhart [15] introduced the notion of basic cohomology as a cohomology theory for the leaf space of a foliation. It has proved to be a powerful and flexible tool in the study of Riemannian foliations. Killing foliations form an important class of Riemannian foliations and are known to possess a type of internal symmetry given by the transverse action of their structural Lie algebras. In order to study this symmetry, Goertsches and Töben [5] introduced the notion of isometric transverse Lie algebra actions on Riemannian foliations and the associated equivariant basic cohomology, and proved, among other things, a Borel-type localization theorem for the transverse actions of structural Lie algebras on Killing foliations. Lin and Sjamaar [10] subsequently studied isometric transverse Lie algebra actions on general Riemannian foliations, established several fundamental properties of such actions, and generalized the Borel-type localization theorem to this broader setting. More recently, Lin and Wang [11] extensively developed equivariant Morse–Bott theory for isometric transverse Lie algebra actions on general Riemannian foliations.

A key tool in classical equivariant geometry is the averaging operator over a compact Lie group, which allows one to replace arbitrary closed differential forms by invariant ones without changing cohomology classes. Historically, this averaging principle plays an important role in the classical study of homogeneous spaces. Building on earlier work of Cartan, Chevalley and Eilenberg identified the de Rham cohomology of G/HG/H with the relative Lie algebra cohomology H∙​(𝔤,𝔥)H^{\bullet}(\mathfrak{g},\mathfrak{h}) when GG is compact and connected and H⊂GH\subset G is closed and connected. In the study of isometric transverse Lie algebra actions, however, there is in general no compact Lie group acting on the foliated manifold MM, and the classical compact-group averaging mechanism is therefore unavailable. The main technical contribution of this paper is the construction of a transverse analogue of this averaging operator for isometric transverse Lie algebra actions on Riemannian foliations. In contrast with the classical averaging operator, our construction is built purely from infinitesimal transverse data and does not require any global group action on the underlying manifold MM.

The main difficulty lies in the absence of a global compact group action on MM. Our approach combines several ingredients from the theory of Riemannian foliations, notably Molino’s structural theory, the framework for isometric transverse Lie algebra actions developed by Lin and Sjamaar, Sergiescu’s transverse integration, and basic Poincaré duality. As a first application of Theorem 1.1, we identify the E1E_{1}-term of the spectral sequence associated to the Cartan complex computing the equivariant basic cohomology H𝔤​(M,ℱ)H_{\mathfrak{g}}(M,\mathcal{F}). In the special case of Killing foliations, for the canonical transverse action of the structural Lie algebra, Goertsches and Töben [5, Thm. 3.23] identified this E1E_{1}-term by using the fact that every basic form is automatically invariant under the action. For a general transverse Lie algebra action, however, they pointed out that the situation is less clear, since Ω​(M,ℱ)𝔤\Omega(M,\mathcal{F})^{\mathfrak{g}} may be a proper subspace of Ω​(M,ℱ)\Omega(M,\mathcal{F}) and no averaging process was available [5, Sec. 3.6]. The transverse averaging operator constructed in Theorem 1.1 resolves this difficulty for isometric transverse Lie algebra actions on complete Riemannian foliations with compact leaf closure space.

Theorem 1.2.

Let (M,ℱ)(M,\mathcal{F}) be a complete Riemannian foliation with compact leaf closure space, equipped with an isometric transverse 𝔤\mathfrak{g}-action. Then the E1E_{1}-term of the spectral sequence associated to the Cartan complex of equivariant basic cohomology is naturally isomorphic to

E1≅(S​𝔤∗)𝔤⊗H∙​(M,ℱ).E_{1}\cong(S\mathfrak{g}^{*})^{\mathfrak{g}}\otimes H^{\bullet}(M,\mathcal{F}).

We say that a Lie 𝔤\mathfrak{g}-foliation is of compact type if the Lie algebra 𝔤\mathfrak{g} is of compact type. Such foliations carry a natural isometric transverse 𝔤\mathfrak{g}-action, so the transverse averaging operator applies. A principal conceptual application of the transverse averaging operator is the following cohomological description of Lie 𝔤\mathfrak{g}-foliations of compact type.

Theorem 1.3.

Let (M,ℱ)(M,\mathcal{F}) be a Lie 𝔤\mathfrak{g}-foliation of compact type with compact leaf closure space. Assume that MM is connected. Then

H∙​(M,ℱ)≅H∙​(𝔤).H^{\bullet}(M,\mathcal{F})\cong H^{\bullet}(\mathfrak{g}).

Although Theorem 1.3 is the more general structural result, its formulation is intrinsically foliated. To obtain concrete applications, we turn to the diffeological de Rham cohomology of homogeneous quotients G/HG/H, where GG is not assumed to be compact and HH is not assumed to be closed. By the theorem of Hector, Macías-Virgós, and Sanmartín-Carbón [12, Theorem 3.5], this cohomology is identified with the basic cohomology of the foliation on GG whose leaves are the right HH-orbits. The key new ingredient is the transverse averaging operator developed in this paper, which makes this basic cohomology computable in the situations considered below. In the ideal case, Theorem 1.3 further sharpens the picture: it leads to a quotient Lie algebra description and allows us to remove the assumption that 𝔤\mathfrak{g} be of compact type. In this sense, the results below extend the classical Chevalley–Eilenberg theorem beyond the classical setting of compact groups and closed subgroups.

Theorem 1.4.

Let GG be a connected Lie group, not necessarily compact, and let HH be a connected Lie subgroup of GG, not necessarily closed. Let 𝔤\mathfrak{g} and 𝔥\mathfrak{h} be the Lie algebras of GG and HH, respectively. Suppose that G/H¯G/\overline{H} is compact and that 𝔤\mathfrak{g} is of compact type. Then the diffeological de Rham cohomology HdR∙​(G/H)H^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{dR}}(G/H) is isomorphic to the relative Lie algebra cohomology H∙​(𝔤,𝔥)H^{\bullet}(\mathfrak{g},\mathfrak{h}).

When 𝔥\mathfrak{h} is an ideal of 𝔤\mathfrak{g}, we obtain a stronger result: it suffices to assume only that G/H¯G/\overline{H} is compact.

Theorem 1.5.

Let GG be a connected Lie group, not necessarily compact, and let HH be a connected Lie subgroup of GG, not necessarily closed. Let 𝔤\mathfrak{g} and 𝔥\mathfrak{h} be the Lie algebras of GG and HH, respectively. Suppose that 𝔥\mathfrak{h} is an ideal of 𝔤\mathfrak{g} and that G/H¯G/\overline{H} is compact. Then the diffeological de Rham cohomology HdR∙​(G/H)H^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{dR}}(G/H) is isomorphic to the Lie algebra cohomology H∙​(𝔤/𝔥)H^{\bullet}(\mathfrak{g}/\mathfrak{h}).

In the special case where HH is connected and dense in GG, Theorem 1.5 recovers a recent result of Clark and Ziegler [4], who proved the same conclusion for an arbitrary dense Lie subgroup HH of GG. Theorem 1.5 may be viewed as a generalization of the classical Chevalley–Eilenberg theorem to a setting in which GG is not necessarily compact and HH is not necessarily closed, under the additional assumption that G/H¯G/\overline{H} is compact. Conceptually, it extends the classical averaging-over-compact-groups mechanism to the transverse setting via the transverse averaging operator constructed in this paper. The full Clark–Ziegler dense-subgroup theorem also admits a direct foliation-theoretic proof, which we give in Theorem A.12. To treat the possibly disconnected dense-subgroup case, we require an equivariant generalization, due to the author, of the theorem of Hector et al. [12, Theorem 5]; this is established in Theorem A.10. It allows one to incorporate the residual action of the component group and thereby recover the full Clark–Ziegler theorem. We emphasize, however, that this equivariant refinement is needed only for this purpose. For the main results of the paper, the diffeological input is minimal and enters essentially only through the formulation of Theorems 1.4 and 1.5.

The paper is organized as follows. Section 2 recalls the necessary background on transverse geometric structures and equivariant basic cohomology. Section 3 reviews Sergiescu’s approach to transverse integration via Molino theory, together with the basic Poincaré duality theorem. Section 4 is devoted to the construction of the transverse averaging operator and the proof of Theorem 1.1, the main technical result of the paper. Section 5 applies the transverse averaging operator to compute the basic cohomology of Lie foliations of compact type. Section 6 proves Theorems 1.4 and 1.5, computing the diffeological de Rham cohomology of the quotient space G/HG/H, where HH is a Lie subgroup of GG, not necessarily closed. Finally, Appendix A collects the diffeological background needed in Section 6 and contains the proof of Theorem A.10, which is used there to give an alternative proof of the full Clark–Ziegler dense subgroup theorem.

2. Transverse geometric structures on foliations

This section reviews the transverse geometric and algebraic background used in the sequel. After fixing notation for transverse vector fields and basic forms, we recall transverse Riemannian metrics, transverse Killing vector fields, and the Cartan model for equivariant basic cohomology. We also review relative Chevalley–Eilenberg cohomology, which will be used in the proof of Theorems 1.4 and 1.5.

Let ℱ\mathcal{F} be a foliation on a smooth manifold MM, and let T​ℱT\mathcal{F} be the tangent bundle of the foliation. Throughout this paper we denote by 𝔛​(ℱ)⊂𝔛​(M)\mathfrak{X}(\mathcal{F})\subset\mathfrak{X}(M) the subspace of vector fields tangent to the leaves of ℱ\mathcal{F}. We say that a vector field X∈𝔛​(M)X\in\mathfrak{X}(M) is foliate, if [X,Y]∈𝔛​(ℱ)[X,Y]\in\mathfrak{X}(\mathcal{F}) for all Y∈𝔛​(ℱ)Y\in\mathfrak{X}(\mathcal{F}). We will denote by ℜ​(ℱ)\mathfrak{R}(\mathcal{F}) the space of foliate vector fields on (M,ℱ)(M,\mathcal{F}). Clearly we have that 𝔛​(ℱ)⊂ℜ​(ℱ)\mathfrak{X}(\mathcal{F})\subset\mathfrak{R}(\mathcal{F}). In this context, a transverse vector field is an equivalence class in the quotient space ℜ​(ℱ)/𝔛​(ℱ)\mathfrak{R}(\mathcal{F})/\mathfrak{X}(\mathcal{F}). The space of transverse vector fields, denoted by 𝔛​(M,ℱ)\mathfrak{X}(M,\mathcal{F}), forms a Lie algebra with a Lie bracket inherited from the natural one on ℜ​(ℱ)\mathfrak{R}(\mathcal{F}). The space of basic forms on MM is

Ω∙​(M,ℱ):={α∈Ω∙​(M)∣ιX​α=ℒX​α=0,∀X∈𝔛​(ℱ)}.\Omega^{\bullet}(M,\mathcal{F}):=\{\alpha\in\Omega^{\bullet}(M)\mid\iota_{X}\alpha=\mathcal{L}_{X}\alpha=0,\ \forall X\in\mathfrak{X}(\mathcal{F})\}.

Since dd preserves basic forms, (Ω∙​(M,ℱ),d)(\Omega^{\bullet}(M,\mathcal{F}),d) is a subcomplex of the de Rham complex. Its cohomology H∙​(M,ℱ)H^{\bullet}(M,\mathcal{F}) is called the basic cohomology.

Definition 2.1.

A transverse Riemannian metric on a foliation (M,ℱ)(M,\mathcal{F}) is a Riemannian metric gg on the normal bundle T​M/T​ℱTM/T\mathcal{F} of the foliation, such that ℒX​g=0\mathcal{L}_{X}g=0, for X∈𝔛​(ℱ)X\in\mathfrak{X}(\mathcal{F}). We say that (ℱ,g)(\mathcal{F},g) is a Riemannian foliation if there exists a transverse Riemannian metric gg on (M,ℱ)(M,\mathcal{F}).

Definition 2.2.

A Riemannian metric gT​Mg_{TM} on a foliated manifold (M,ℱ)(M,\mathcal{F}) is bundle-like if for every open set U⊂MU\subset M and every pair of foliate vector fields v,wv,w on UU that are orthogonal to the leaves, the function gT​M​(v,w)g_{TM}(v,w) is basic on UU.

A bundle-like Riemannian metric gT​Mg_{TM} on (M,ℱ)(M,\mathcal{F}) induces a transverse Riemannian metric gg by identifying N​ℱN\mathcal{F} with the gT​Mg_{TM}-orthogonal complement of T​ℱT\mathcal{F} and restricting gT​Mg_{TM} to N​ℱN\mathcal{F}. Conversely, every transverse Riemannian metric arises in this way from a bundle-like Riemannian metric. We say that a Riemannian foliation (M,ℱ,g)(M,\mathcal{F},g) is complete if there exists a complete bundle-like Riemannian metric on MM inducing gg.

Let (M,ℱ)(M,\mathcal{F}) be a Riemannian foliation with transverse metric gg, and let X¯∈𝔛​(M,ℱ)\overline{X}\in\mathfrak{X}(M,\mathcal{F}) be a transverse vector field represented by a foliate vector field XX. Define ℒX¯​g:=ℒX​g\mathcal{L}_{\overline{X}}g:=\mathcal{L}_{X}g. This is well defined, i.e. independent of the choice of representative XX. We say that X¯\overline{X} is transversely Killing if ℒX¯​g=0\mathcal{L}_{\overline{X}}g=0. If X¯\overline{X} and Y¯\overline{Y} are transversely Killing, then the Cartan identities imply that [X¯,Y¯][\overline{X},\overline{Y}] is again transversely Killing. Thus the space of transversely Killing vector fields, denoted by iso​(M,ℱ)\mathrm{iso}(M,\mathcal{F}), is a Lie subalgebra of 𝔛​(M,ℱ)\mathfrak{X}(M,\mathcal{F}), called the transverse Killing Lie algebra.

Historically, the conclusion of Theorem 1.3 appears to be well known in the case where a complete Lie foliation ℱ\mathcal{F} on a manifold MM has a dense leaf. Let GG be the simply connected Lie group with Lie algebra 𝔤\mathfrak{g}. By Fedida’s Darboux covering construction for a complete Lie 𝔤\mathfrak{g}-foliation (see [14, Chapter 4.2]), the basic de Rham complex (Ω∙​(M,ℱ),d)(\Omega^{\bullet}(M,\mathcal{F}),d) is naturally identified with (Ω∙​(G)Γ,d)(\Omega^{\bullet}(G)^{\Gamma},d), where Γ\Gamma is a discrete subgroup of GG—called the holonomy group of the Darboux covering—acting on GG on the left. When ℱ\mathcal{F} has a dense leaf, Γ\Gamma is dense in GG, and a continuity argument yields

(Ω∙​(M,ℱ),d)≅(Ω∙​(G)Γ,d)=(Ω∙​(G)G,d)=(CC​E∙​(𝔤),d),(\Omega^{\bullet}(M,\mathcal{F}),d)\;\cong\;(\Omega^{\bullet}(G)^{\Gamma},d)\;=\;(\Omega^{\bullet}(G)^{G},d)\;=\;(C_{CE}^{\bullet}(\mathfrak{g}),d),

so the basic cohomology of ℱ\mathcal{F} coincides with H∙​(𝔤)H^{\bullet}(\mathfrak{g}).

A second case, perhaps less well known and apparently not recorded in the literature, arises when 𝔤\mathfrak{g} is semisimple of compact type. Then 𝔤\mathfrak{g} integrates to a compact simply connected Lie group GG, and H:=Γ¯H:=\overline{\Gamma} is a compact and connected subgroup of GG. Since Ω∙​(G)Γ=Ω∙​(G)H\Omega^{\bullet}(G)^{\Gamma}=\Omega^{\bullet}(G)^{H}, averaging over the compact group HH shows that

H∙​(Ω∙​(G)H,d)≅H∙​(𝔤).H^{\bullet}(\Omega^{\bullet}(G)^{H},d)\cong H^{\bullet}(\mathfrak{g}).

The main novelty of Theorem 1.3 lies in the general compact type case, where 𝔤\mathfrak{g} may possess a nontrivial center.

L=PSL2​(ℝ),L=\mathrm{PSL}_{2}(\mathbb{R}),

and let Γ⊂L\Gamma\subset L be a torsion-free cocompact lattice. Set

and let ℱ\mathcal{F} be the point foliation on MM. Then (M,ℱ)(M,\mathcal{F}) is a Lie 𝔩\mathfrak{l}-foliation, where 𝔩=Lie⁡(L)=𝔰​𝔩2​(ℝ)\mathfrak{l}=\operatorname{Lie}(L)=\mathfrak{sl}_{2}(\mathbb{R}).

Indeed, the right Maurer–Cartan form on LL is left-invariant, hence descends to an 𝔩\mathfrak{l}-valued 11-form on MM. Since ℱ\mathcal{F} is the point foliation, this descended form has kernel 0=T​ℱ0=T\mathcal{F}, is pointwise an isomorphism, and satisfies the Maurer–Cartan equation.

The leaf closure space of (M,ℱ)(M,\mathcal{F}) is just MM itself, and is therefore compact. On the other hand, 𝔩=𝔰​𝔩2​(ℝ)\mathfrak{l}=\mathfrak{sl}_{2}(\mathbb{R}) is not of compact type.

Since ℱ\mathcal{F} is the point foliation, its basic cohomology coincides with ordinary de Rham cohomology:

H∙​(M,ℱ)=HdR∙​(M).H^{\bullet}(M,\mathcal{F})=H^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{dR}}(M).

Moreover, MM is diffeomorphic to the unit tangent bundle T1​ΣT^{1}\Sigma of a closed hyperbolic surface Σ\Sigma. Hence, by the Gysin sequence of the circle bundle

we have

H1​(M,ℱ)=HdR1​(M)≠0.H^{1}(M,\mathcal{F})=H^{1}_{\mathrm{dR}}(M)\neq 0.

By contrast, Whitehead’s lemma gives

H1​(𝔩)=H1​(𝔰​𝔩2​(ℝ))=0.H^{1}(\mathfrak{l})=H^{1}(\mathfrak{sl}_{2}(\mathbb{R}))=0.

Therefore

H∙​(M,ℱ)≇H∙​(𝔩).H^{\bullet}(M,\mathcal{F})\not\cong H^{\bullet}(\mathfrak{l}).

Thus the conclusion of Theorem 1.3 fails without the compact-type hypothesis, even in the case of the point foliation.

6. The de Rham cohomology of G/HG/H

Throughout this section, we use LL and RR to denote left and right group actions, respectively. We write 𝔤\mathfrak{g} and 𝔥\mathfrak{h} for the Lie algebras of a Lie group GG and its Lie subgroup HH, respectively, and we let ℱ\mathcal{F} denote the foliation on GG whose leaves are the orbits of the right action of HH on GG. The main results of this section are Theorems 1.4 and 1.5. A theorem of Héctor, Macías-Virgós, and Sanmartín-Carbón [12, Theorem 3.5] identifies the basic cohomology of a foliation with the diffeological de Rham cohomology of its leaf space. In the present setting, the leaf space is precisely the diffeological quotient G/HG/H. Thus the problem is reduced to one in basic cohomology. The key new ingredient is the transverse averaging mechanism developed earlier. We begin with a technical lemma.

Lemma 6.1.

HdR∙​(G/H)≅H∙​(G,ℱ)≅H∙​(Ω​(G,ℱ)L​(G),d)≅H∙​(𝔤,𝔥).H^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{dR}}(G/H)\cong H^{\bullet}(G,\mathcal{F})\cong H^{\bullet}(\Omega(G,\mathcal{F})^{L(G)},d)\cong H^{\bullet}(\mathfrak{g},\mathfrak{h}).

The following result shows that when 𝔥\mathfrak{h} is an ideal of 𝔤\mathfrak{g}, the compactness assumption on G/H¯G/\overline{H} in Theorem 1.5 automatically implies that 𝔤/𝔥\mathfrak{g}/\mathfrak{h} is of compact type.

Suppose that GG is connected, HH is a connected Lie subgroup of GG, 𝔥\mathfrak{h} is an ideal of 𝔤\mathfrak{g}, and G/H¯G/\overline{H} is compact. Then (G,ℱ)(G,\mathcal{F}) is a complete Lie (𝔤/𝔥)(\mathfrak{g}/\mathfrak{h})-foliation of compact type.

We are now ready to prove Theorem 1.5.

Proof of Theorem 1.5.

Note that the leaf space of the foliation ℱ\mathcal{F} is precisely the homogeneous space G/HG/H. It follows from [12, Theorem 3.5] that the diffeological de Rham cohomology HdR∙​(G/H)H^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{dR}}(G/H) is canonically isomorphic to the basic cohomology H∙​(G,ℱ)H^{\bullet}(G,\mathcal{F}).

HdR∙​(G/H)≅H∙​(𝔤/𝔥).H^{\bullet}_{\mathrm{dR}}(G/H)\cong H^{\bullet}(\mathfrak{g}/\mathfrak{h}).

Appendix A De Rham cohomology of a diffeological space

This appendix collects the diffeological background used in the paper; for general background on diffeology, we refer the reader to [7]. For the main body, only the result of Hector, Macías-Virgós, and Sanmartín-Carbón [12] is needed, together with a minimal amount of diffeological language. The main new results of the appendix are Proposition A.8 and Theorem A.10. These are not needed for the proofs of Theorems 1.4 and 1.5, but are used later to give an alternative foliation-theoretic proof of the Clark–Ziegler theorem, and may also be of independent interest.

Theorem A.10 establishes an equivariant version of that result: for any regular foliation (M,ℱ)(M,\mathcal{F}), there is a canonical isomorphism of cochain complexes

π∗:(Ω∙​(M/ℱ,𝒟′),d)→∼(Ω∙​(M,ℱ),d),\pi^{*}\colon(\Omega^{\bullet}(M/\mathcal{F},\mathcal{D}^{\prime}),d)\xrightarrow{\;\sim\;}(\Omega^{\bullet}(M,\mathcal{F}),d),

where M/ℱM/\mathcal{F} is equipped with the quotient diffeology 𝒟′\mathcal{D}^{\prime}. Our proof replaces the indirect holonomy pseudogroup argument with one based on Haefliger’s cocycle definition, which makes equivariance transparent in the presence of a foliation-preserving action of a Lie group HH. Proposition A.8 identifies the diffeological de Rham cohomology of a quotient X/KX/K of a diffeological space XX by a countable group KK with the cohomology of the complex of KK-invariant diffeological forms on XX. For an alternative proof of the original result of Hector et al. using Lie groupoid techniques, together with an extension to certain classes of singular foliations, including singular Riemannian foliations, we refer the reader to [13].

Theorem A.12.

Let HH be a dense Lie subgroup of a Lie group GG, and let 𝔥\mathfrak{h} and 𝔤\mathfrak{g} be the Lie algebras of HH and GG, respectively. Let ℱ\mathcal{F} be the foliation on GG whose leaves are the orbits of the right H0H_{0}-action, and let 𝒟H\mathcal{D}_{H} be the quotient diffeology on G/HG/H.

H∙​(G/H,𝒟H)≅H∙​(𝔤/𝔥).H^{\bullet}(G/H,\mathcal{D}_{H})\cong H^{\bullet}(\mathfrak{g}/\mathfrak{h}).
