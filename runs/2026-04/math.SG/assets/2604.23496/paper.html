Q-Manifolds and Sigma Models

Abstract.

Recent developments of Batalin-Vilkovisky (BV) formalism and related geometry are reviewed. Mathematical structures of BV formalism are summarized as a Q-manifold and a QP-manifold. Lie algebras, Lie algebroids and other higher algebroids are explained as typical examples of Q- and QP-manifolds. Finally, the BV action functionals are constructed by geometric structures of Lie algebroids and Courant algebroids.

Key words and phrases:
Graded manifolds, Batalin-Vilkovisky formalism, Algebroids and groupoids

1. Introduction

Batalin-Vilkovisky (BV) formalism of gauge theories is a canonical formulation for quantization of a classical field theory with gauge symmetries. In the BRST-BV quantization in a gauge theory, the so-called ghost fields which are odd or even variables, are introduced.

Graded manifolds, especially, Q-manifolds and QP-manifolds are mathematical basis of BV formalism.

A BV action functional SB​VS_{BV} is a homological function generating BRST transformations and carries all the physical information. However, the construction of the BV action functional SB​VS_{BV} is not easy. Especially, geometrical interpretation of SB​VS_{BV} is not well understood so far. After reviewing BV formalism and introducing Q- and QP-manifolds, relations between Q- and QP-manifolds and underling geometry are discussed. Based on these observations, BV action functionals SB​VS_{BV} are constructed by the geometric quantities such as torsions and curvatures.

2. Necessity of BV Formalism

Here, necessity of the BV formalism is explained in a basic example.

For a gauge field AA, the action functional of the Yang-Mills theory on a four-dimensional manifold Σ4\Sigma_{4} is

S=−14∫Σ4tr(F∧∗F),\displaystyle S=-\frac{1}{4}\int_{\Sigma_{4}}\mathrm{tr}(F\wedge*F), (2.1)

where F=d​A+A∧AF=\mathrm{d}A+A\wedge A is a field strength. Here A=Aμa​d​σμ​eaA=A_{\mu}^{a}\mathrm{d}\sigma^{\mu}e_{a} is a connection 11-form taking value on a Lie algebra 𝔤\mathfrak{g}. σμ\sigma^{\mu} is a local coordinate on Σ4\Sigma_{4}, and eae_{a} is a basis of 𝔤\mathfrak{g}. Applying the variational principle to the action functional SS, Euler-Lagrange equations which give equations of motion of physics are calculated.

The action functional SS has gauge invariance under the following infinitesimal gauge transformation:

δϵ​A=d​ϵ+[A,ϵ],\displaystyle\delta_{\epsilon}A=\mathrm{d}\epsilon+[A,\epsilon], (2.2)

where ϵ∈C∞​(Σ4,𝔤)\epsilon\in C^{\infty}(\Sigma_{4},\mathfrak{g}) is a gauge parameter, which is a function on Σ4\Sigma_{4} taking value on a Lie algebra 𝔤\mathfrak{g}. The gauge invariance means that that action functional is invariant under the gauge transformation (2.2) δϵ​S=0\delta_{\epsilon}S=0 for every ϵ\epsilon. A set of gauge transformations is a closed Lie algebra under the commutators of transformations,

[δϵ,δϵ′]​A=δ[ϵ,ϵ′]​A,(off-shell)\displaystyle[\delta_{\epsilon},\delta_{\epsilon^{\prime}}]A=\delta_{[\epsilon,\epsilon^{\prime}]}A,\qquad\mbox{(off-shell)}

without using equations of motion, where [δϵ,δϵ′]=δϵ​δϵ′−δϵ′​δϵ[\delta_{\epsilon},\delta_{\epsilon^{\prime}}]=\delta_{\epsilon}\delta_{\epsilon^{\prime}}-\delta_{\epsilon^{\prime}}\delta_{\epsilon}.

The BRST quantization is a standard quantization procedure of the Yang-Mills theory. There are a lot of historical developments like gauge fixings, Gupla-Bleuler, Faddev-Popov, Becchi-Rouet-Stora-Tyutin (BRST), Kugo-Ojima, etc. One can refer to some textbooks of a quantum field theory, for instance, [30].

We construct an operator of which square is zero and induced from the gauge transformation and make a consistent quantization by considering a cohomology called the BRST cohomology. For it, change a gauge parameter ϵ​(σ)\epsilon(\sigma) to a Grassmann odd field called the Faddeev-Popov (FP) ghost c​(σ)c(\sigma). The corresponding transformation is denoted by ss,

s​A=d​c+[A,c].\displaystyle sA=\mathrm{d}c+[A,c].

The action functional is invariant under the BRST transformation s​S=0sS=0. Ghost numbers are denoted by gh{\rm gh} and defined as gh​A=0{\rm gh}\,A=0 and gh​c=1{\rm gh}\,c=1. Then, ss is of ghost number one. If we define s​c=−12​[c,c]sc=-\frac{1}{2}[c,c], then we obtain s2​A=s2​c=0s^{2}A=s^{2}c=0 without using equations of motion.

In order to make the gauge fixing, we introduce the Grassmann odd antighosts c¯\bar{c} and the Grassmann evern Nakanishi-Lautrap field bb satisfying the BRST transformations, s​c¯=i​bs\bar{c}=ib and s​b=0sb=0. The gauge fixed action functional is given by adding gauge fixing terms to the action functional SS under the requirement s​Sq=0sS_{q}=0. It is

Sq\displaystyle S_{q} =\displaystyle= ∫Σ(tr(F∧∗F)+b∗d∗A+∗α2tr(bb)−ic¯∗d∗Dc).\displaystyle\int_{\Sigma}\left(\mathrm{tr}(F\wedge*F)+b*\mathrm{d}*A+*\frac{\alpha}{2}\mathrm{tr}(bb)-i\bar{c}*\mathrm{d}*Dc\right). (2.3)

Here α\alpha is a constant, and ∗* is the Hodge star. For consistency of the quantization after the gauge fixing, SqS_{q} must be invariant under the BRST transformation, s​Sq=0sS_{q}=0. It is concretely checked in Eq. (2.3). The partition function,

Z\displaystyle Z =\displaystyle= ∫𝒟​A​𝒟​b​𝒟​c​𝒟​c¯​eiℏ​Sq,\displaystyle\int\mathcal{D}A\mathcal{D}b\mathcal{D}c\mathcal{D}\bar{c}e^{\frac{i}{\hbar}S_{q}}, (2.4)

is consistent with BRST (gauge) transformations. Physical states are defined by the cohomology class of the complex of all fields and ghosts, where the differential QB​R​S​TQ_{BRST} is one induced from the BRST transformation ss. It means that physical states |phys⟩|\mbox{phys}\rangle are cohomology classes satisfying QB​R​S​T​|phys⟩=0Q_{BRST}|\mbox{phys}\rangle=0, where QB​R​S​TQ_{BRST} a Grassmann odd charge operator for BRST transformations [27]. Note that QB​R​S​TQ_{BRST} is Grassmann odd and QB​R​S​T2=0Q_{BRST}^{2}=0. Since QB​R​S​T2=0Q_{BRST}^{2}=0 without EOMs, the condition QB​R​S​T​|phys⟩=0Q_{BRST}|\mbox{phys}\rangle=0 is consistent.

In general constraint systems in the Hamiltonian mechanics, or equivalently, general gauge invariant Lagrangian mechanics, general consistency conditions of a gauge invariant classical theory are

δϵ​S=0(off-shell),[δϵ,δϵ′]=δ[ϵ,ϵ′]+(equations of motion).\displaystyle\delta_{\epsilon}S=0\ \ (\mbox{\rm off-shell}),\qquad[\delta_{\epsilon},\delta_{\epsilon^{\prime}}]=\delta_{[\epsilon,\epsilon^{\prime}]}+(\mbox{\rm equations of motion}). (2.5)

Here, “off shell” means without using EOMs. Especially, the theory is still consistent if a gauge algebra is closed using EOMs. In this theory, the BRST transformation satisfies

s​S=0(off-shell),s2=(equations of motion).\displaystyle sS=0\ \ (\mbox{\rm off-shell}),\qquad s^{2}=(\mbox{\rm equations of motion}). (2.6)

The BRST quantization procedure does not work in this case. If we take gauge fixing for the quantization, gauge fixing terms are added in the action functional by introducing ghosts, antighosts, etc. The gauge fixed action functional SqS_{q} changes the EOMs from the classical action functional SS. Thus, s2=(equations of motion)s^{2}=(\mbox{\rm equations of motion}) does not hold after gauge fixing. In general, corresponding BRST operator QB​R​S​TQ_{BRST} does not satisfy QB​R​S​T2=0Q_{BRST}^{2}=0, and the physical condition in the BRST formalism QB​R​S​T​|phys⟩=0Q_{BRST}|\mbox{phys}\rangle=0 is inconsistent!

BV formalism makes the formulation consistent, and it is the procedure to obtain an operator QQ such that Q2=0Q^{2}=0 without EOMs.

3. Batalin-Vilkovisky (BV) Formalism

In this section, we explain BV formalism [3, 4], especially, construction of a BV action functional SB​VS_{BV} from a classical action functional SS satisfying

s​SB​V=0,(off-shell),s2=0,(off-shell),\displaystyle sS_{BV}=0,\ \ (\mbox{\rm off-shell}),\qquad s^{2}=0,\ \ (\mbox{\rm off-shell}),

without changing physics.

For it, more auxiliary fields called antifields are introduced.

3.1. Poisson Sigma Model

We explain the procedure of the BV formalism by an example, the Poisson sigma model (PSM) [17, 33], which needs BV formalism for the quantization.

The Poisson sigma model is a sigma model from Σ\Sigma in two dimensions to a target manifold MM in dd dimensions. Let σμ\sigma^{\mu} be a local coordinate on Σ\Sigma and xix^{i} be a local coordinate on MM. i=1,…,di=1,\ldots,d are target space indices. Precisely, we consider the space of vector bundle morphisms Hom​(T​Σ,T∗​M){\rm Hom}(T\Sigma,T^{*}M) between T​ΣT\Sigma and T∗​MT^{*}M.

where ⟨−,−⟩\langle-,\,-\rangle is the pairing of T​MTM and T∗​MT^{*}M, π∈Γ​(∧2T​M)\pi\in\Gamma(\wedge^{2}TM) is a bivector field on MM and dΣ\mathrm{d}_{\Sigma} is the de Rham differential on Σ\Sigma. πi​j​(X)=−πj​i​(X)\pi^{ij}(X)=-\pi^{ji}(X) is an antisymmetric tensor.

This model includes important examples in physics as two-dimensional dilaton gravity (a deformation of the JT gravity) [21, 13], topological string theories, A-model, B-model [37, 38], BF-type topological field theories [5], etc.

3.3. Quantum BV Formalism

Though we do not discuss the quantum BV formalism in this article, it is briefly explained in this section.

In the path integral quantization, we calculate the partition function,

Z=∫ℒ𝒟​Φ​eiℏ​SB​V​q,\displaystyle Z=\int_{\mathcal{L}}\mathcal{D}\Phi\ e^{\frac{i}{\hbar}S_{BVq}}, (3.13)

SB​V​qS_{BVq} is the quantum action functional adding antighost c¯\bar{c}, the NL field bb, and their antifields. Choice of ℒ\mathcal{L} corresponds to gauge fixing. The partition function ZZ must be invariant under infinitesimal changing of the Lagrangian submanifold, ℒ′=ℒ+δ​ℒ\mathcal{L}^{\prime}=\mathcal{L}+\delta\mathcal{L}, which means that the partition function ZZ must not depend on choice of gauge. The condition induces the following condition for SB​V​qS_{BVq} called the quantum master equation,

−2​i​ℏ​Δ​SB​V​q+{SB​V​q,SB​V​q}=0.\displaystyle-2i\hbar\Delta S_{BVq}+{\{{{S_{BVq}},{S_{BVq}}}\}}=0. (3.14)

4. Q-Manifolds and QP-Manifolds

Mathematical structures in BV formalism in the physical theory in the previous section 3.2 are summarized as a Q-manifold or a QP-manifold.

BV formalism consists of three structures. Fields, ghosts, and antifields are formulated as a graded manifold. A BV bracket is an odd Poisson bracket induced from a graded symplectic form. A BV action functional and the classical master equation are homological vector field and its Hamiltonian function. Three structures are called a QP-manifold, or a differential graded (dg) symplectic manifold.

Definition 4.1.

A graded manifold ℳ=(M,𝒪M)\mathcal{M}=(M,\mathcal{O}_{M}) on a smooth manifold MM is a ringed space which structure sheaf 𝒪M\mathcal{O}_{M} is ℤ\mathbb{Z}–graded commutative algebras over MM, locally isomorphic to C∞​(U)⊗S∙​(V)C^{\infty}(U)\otimes S^{\bullet}(V), where UU is a local chart on MM, VV is a graded vector space, and S∙​(V)S^{\bullet}(V) is a free graded commutative ring on VV.

Grading is called degree. We denote the structure sheaf by 𝒪M=C∞​(ℳ)\mathcal{O}_{M}=C^{\infty}(\mathcal{M}) and degree of a homogeneous element ff by |f||f|. If degrees are nonnegative, a graded manifold is called a N-manifold.

The product of two homogeneous elements qq and pp of a graded manifold satisfies q​p=(−)|q|​|p|​p​qqp=(-)^{|q||p|}pq.

A derivation on C∞​(ℳ)C^{\infty}(\mathcal{M}), i.e., a map on C∞​(ℳ)C^{\infty}(\mathcal{M}) satisfying the Leibniz rule is called a graded vector field. A graded vector field QQ is called a homological vector field if it is of degree +1+1 and Q2=0Q^{2}=0.

Definition 4.2.

A graded manifold with a homological vector field (ℳ,Q)(\mathcal{M},Q) is called a Q-manifold, or differential graded (dg) manifold.

A graded 11-form is also defined as a map from the space of graded vector fields to C∞​(ℳ)C^{\infty}(\mathcal{M}), and a graded differential form is defined by taking graded wedge products.

9. Outlook

We have discussed construction of a classical BV action functional based on underlining geometry. Concrete constructions for higher n≥3n\geq 3 are unknown. However, the EE-torsion and the basic curvature have important roles. QP-manifolds of mapping spaces, which appear in BV formalisms of general gauge theories have more complicated structures. They have many analyses but still are not well understood yet.

We have not discussed the quantization. To analyze relations of the quantization and geometry are an important problem.
