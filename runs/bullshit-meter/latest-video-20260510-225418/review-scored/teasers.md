| 1 | 6.2 | [bm-319-01](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25709) |  | Perturbative unitarity of fractional field theories and gravity | This paper attempts to show that a proposed theory of quantum gravity, built from fractional (non-integer power) versions of the wave operator, can be made mathematically consistent in the sense that probabilities are conserved across particle interactions. The review board found the tree-level spectral analysis — a detailed classification of which modes appear in the propagator and on which mathematical sheets — to be a genuine and useful technical contribution. However, the central claim of unitarity at *all* perturbative orders is extrapolated from one-loop examples without a rigorous inductive proof, and the extension to gauge and gravitational theories omits the BRST/ghost framework that such proofs require. |
