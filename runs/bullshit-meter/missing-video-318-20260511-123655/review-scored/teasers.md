| 1 | 2 | [bm-318-01](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.06328) |  | On computing quantum waves exactly from classical and relativistic action | This paper from MIT claims that quantum wave functions can be derived exactly from classical particle paths alone — no Feynman "sum over all histories" needed — by combining multi-valued classical action solutions with classical fluid density. The review board found the individual examples correct but the central proof fatally flawed: it silently assumes a condition that does not hold in general, and the paper's treatment of the Bell inequality experiment substitutes continuous spinor math for the discrete binary measurements that Bell's theorem actually governs. All worked examples succeed for reasons already known to physics, not because the general framework is valid. |
